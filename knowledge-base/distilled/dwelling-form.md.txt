# NFIP Standard Flood Insurance Policy -- Dwelling Form

**Form F-122 / October 2021**

Applies to: 1-4 family residential buildings (not condos under association form), single-family condo units, and personal property in a building. One building per policy.

---

## Coverage A -- Building Property

### What's Covered

- The dwelling at the described location
- Additions/extensions attached by rigid exterior wall, load-bearing interior wall, stairway, elevated walkway, or roof
- **Detached garage**: Limited to **10% of building coverage limit**. Must not be used for residential, business, or farming purposes. Uses reduce the building limit.
- Materials and supplies for construction/alteration/repair, stored in a fully enclosed building at the described location or adjacent property
- Building under construction, alteration, or repair at the described location
- Manufactured homes and travel trailers (must be on permanent foundation; if in SFHA, must be anchored per manufacturer specs, over-the-top/frame ties, or community requirements)

### Items Covered Under Coverage A Only (Not Personal Property)

Awnings, canopies, blinds, built-in dishwashers, built-in microwaves, permanently installed carpet over unfinished flooring, central air conditioners, elevator equipment, fire sprinkler systems, walk-in freezers, furnaces, radiators, garbage disposals, hot water heaters (including solar), light fixtures, outdoor antennas fastened to building, permanently installed cupboards/bookcases/cabinets/paneling/wallpaper, plumbing fixtures, pumps and pump machinery, ranges/stoves/ovens, refrigerators, permanently installed wall mirrors.

### Basement and Below-Elevated-Floor Coverage (Severely Limited)

In a **basement** (any zone) or **below the lowest elevated floor** of an elevated post-FIRM building in zones A1-A30, AE, AH, AR, AR/A, AR/AE, AR/AH, AR/A1-A30, V1-V30, VE, coverage is limited to:

- Central air conditioners
- Cisterns and water in them
- Drywall for walls/ceilings (unfinished, unfloated, not taped, nailed to framing only)
- Electrical junction/circuit breaker boxes
- Electrical outlets and switches
- Elevators, dumbwaiters, related equipment (not if installed below BFE after 9/30/1987)
- Fuel tanks and fuel
- Furnaces and hot water heaters
- Heat pumps
- Nonflammable insulation (basement only)
- Solar energy pumps and tanks
- Stairways/staircases attached to building (not separated by elevated walkways)
- Sump pumps
- Water softeners, chemicals, water filters, faucets integral to plumbing
- Well water tanks and pumps
- Required utility connections for above items
- Footings, foundations, posts, pilings, piers, foundation walls, anchorage systems
- **Clean-up**

**Everything else below the elevated floor or in a basement is NOT covered under Coverage A.**

---

## Coverage B -- Personal Property

### What's Covered

Personal property inside a building at the described location, owned by you or household family members. Optionally covers property owned by guests or servants.

Property in a building not fully enclosed must be secured to prevent flotation. If it floats out, coverage is conclusively denied.

### Items Covered Under Coverage B Only

- Portable/window air conditioning units
- Carpets not permanently installed over unfinished flooring
- Carpets over finished flooring
- Clothes washers and dryers
- Cook-out grills
- Food freezers (not walk-in) and food in any freezer
- Portable microwaves and portable dishwashers

### Basement/Below-Elevated-Floor Personal Property (Severely Limited)

Same zone restrictions as Coverage A. Only these items covered:

- Portable/window air conditioning units
- Clothes washers and dryers
- Food freezers (not walk-in) and food in any freezer

### Tenant Coverage

If you are a tenant with Coverage B, policy also covers your cooking stove/range and refrigerator, plus improvements you made at your own expense -- limited to **10% of personal property limit**. Reduces personal property limit.

### Condo Unit Owner Coverage

If you own a condo unit with Coverage B, policy also covers your interior walls, floor, and ceiling (not covered by association policy) -- limited to **10% of personal property limit**. Reduces personal property limit.

### Special Limits -- $2,500 per loss

The following categories are each capped at **$2,500 per loss**:

- Artwork, photographs, collectibles, memorabilia, porcelain figures, sports cards
- Rare books or autographed items
- Jewelry, watches, precious/semi-precious stones, gold/silver/platinum articles
- Furs or articles where fur is principal value
- Personal property used in any business

### Antiques

Only **functional value** is paid -- not collectible or antique premium value.

---

## Coverage C -- Other Coverages

### Debris Removal

- Covers expense to remove non-owned debris on/in insured property, and debris of insured property anywhere
- If you do the work, valued at Federal minimum wage
- Does NOT increase Coverage A or B limits

### Loss Avoidance -- Sandbags, Supplies, and Labor

- Up to **$1,000** for protecting building from flood or imminent flood danger
- Covers: sandbags, sand, fill for temporary levees, pumps, plastic sheeting, lumber
- Your labor valued at Federal minimum wage
- Requires: flood damage must be imminent AND either (a) general flooding in the area occurs, or (b) a legally authorized official issues an evacuation/preservation order
- Does NOT increase Coverage A or B limits

### Loss Avoidance -- Property Removed to Safety

- Up to **$1,000** for reasonable expenses to move insured property to protect from flood
- Coverage continues at new location for **45 consecutive days** from date you begin moving
- Moved property must be in a fully enclosed building or reasonably protected from elements
- Must be placed above ground level or outside SFHA
- Does NOT increase Coverage A or B limits

### Condominium Loss Assessments

- Pays your share of association loss assessments due to flood damage to unit or common elements during policy term, up to Coverage A limit
- Will NOT pay assessments: charged by government bodies; resulting from association's deductible; for personal property losses; exceeding $250,000 combined with RCBAP; or for items already paid under RCBAP

---

## Coverage D -- Increased Cost of Compliance (ICC)

- Limit: **$30,000** -- in addition to Coverage A, but combined Coverage A + D cannot exceed the statutory maximum
- No separate deductible for ICC
- Pays to comply with state/local floodplain management law after flood damage
- Eligible activities: elevation, floodproofing (non-residential only, or residential basements meeting 44 CFR 60.6), relocation, demolition

### Eligibility Triggers

Either:
1. **Repetitive loss building**: Two flood losses in 10 years, average repair cost >= 25% of market value each time, prior claim paid by NFIP, and community enforces repetitive loss provisions; OR
2. **Substantial damage**: Single flood where repair cost >= 50% of market value, and community enforces substantial damage provisions

### ICC Will NOT Pay For

- Emergency Program communities
- Pollutant testing/cleanup costs
- Loss in building value due to ordinance requirements
- Residual value of undamaged portion if demolished
- Code upgrades not related to floodplain management (plumbing, electrical, etc.)
- Compliance for additions/improvements made after the loss
- Pre-existing compliance obligations
- Rebuilding below minimum NFIP standards or with a variance below BFE
- Garages or carports
- Group Flood Insurance properties
- Condo association assessments for common building compliance

### ICC Timing

Must complete elevation/floodproofing/demolition/relocation as soon as reasonably possible, not to exceed **2 years**.

---

## Property NOT Insured

1. Personal property not inside a building
2. Buildings/contents entirely in/on/over water or seaward of mean high tide (if built/improved after 9/30/1982)
3. Open structures, boathouses, structures into which boats float
4. Recreational vehicles (except qualifying travel trailers on permanent foundations)
5. Self-propelled vehicles/machines (exception: unlicensed vehicles used to service described location, or handicapped-assist devices inside building)
6. Land, lawns, trees, shrubs, plants, growing crops, animals
7. Currency, coins, deeds, securities, bullion, manuscripts, stamps, stored value cards, valuable papers
8. Underground structures: wells, septic tanks, septic systems
9. Walks, walkways, decks, driveways, patios outside perimeter exterior walls
10. Gas/liquid storage containers and related equipment
11. Buildings/contents where >49% of ACV is below ground (unless lowest level is at/above BFE and below ground only for energy-efficient insulation)
12. Fences, retaining walls, seawalls, bulkheads, wharves, piers, bridges, docks
13. Aircraft, watercraft, and their furnishings/equipment
14. Hot tubs, spas (not bathroom fixtures), swimming pools and their equipment
15. Property ineligible under Coastal Barrier Resources Act
16. Personal property owned in common with other condo unit owners

---

## Exclusions

### No Coverage For These Loss Types

- Loss of revenue, profits, access, use
- Business interruption
- Additional living expenses during repairs
- Ordinance/law compliance costs (except Coverage D)
- Any other economic loss

### Flood in Progress

No coverage for a flood that was already occurring when the policy became effective or when the application was submitted.

### Earth Movement

Not covered even if caused by flood: earthquake, landslide, land subsidence, sinkholes, destabilization from subsurface water, gradual erosion. **Exception**: mudflow and erosion-caused land subsidence along shorelines ARE covered under the flood definition.

### Other Excluded Causes

- Ice pressure/weight
- Freezing or thawing
- Rain, snow, sleet, hail, water spray (unless flood is proximate cause)
- Water damage from conditions substantially confined to the dwelling or within your control (design defects, broken pipes/fixtures, failure to maintain after flood recedes)
- Sewer/drain backup, sump pump discharge, seepage -- **UNLESS flood in the area is the proximate cause**
- Water pressure/weight -- **UNLESS flood in the area is the proximate cause**
- Power/heating/cooling failure -- unless flood directly damaged that equipment on-site
- Theft, fire, explosion, wind, windstorm
- Intentional acts by insured
- Alterations that significantly increase flood risk

---

## Deductibles

- Separate deductibles apply to building and personal property in each loss
- Amount shown on Declarations Page
- **Building under construction** without two rigid exterior walls and fully secured roof: deductible is **2x** the normal amount
- Deductible does NOT apply to: Loss Avoidance Measures, Condominium Loss Assessments, or ICC (Coverage D)

---

## Loss Settlement

### Method 1: Replacement Cost (Best for Insured)

Applies to single-family dwellings that are:
- Your **principal residence** (lived there 80%+ of the preceding 365 days or ownership period)
- Insured for **80% or more of full replacement cost** OR at the maximum NFIP coverage limit

Payment = least of: (a) policy building limit, (b) replacement cost with like kind/quality materials, (c) amount actually spent to repair/replace.

Key rules:
- No deduction for depreciation
- If rebuilt at new location, cost limited to what it would have cost at former location
- **If repair/replacement cost exceeds $1,000 or 5% of total building coverage, payment is not made until actual repair/replacement is completed**
- You may initially claim on ACV basis, then claim the replacement cost difference within **180 days** of loss

### Method 2: Special Loss Settlement (Manufactured/Mobile Homes)

Applies to manufactured homes, mobile homes, or travel trailers that are: at least 16 feet wide, at least 600 sq ft, and your principal residence.

- **Total loss or not economically feasible to repair**: pays lesser of replacement cost or 1.5x ACV, capped at policy limit
- **Partial damage, feasible to repair**: uses Replacement Cost method above

### Method 3: Actual Cash Value (ACV)

Applies to:
- Dwellings insured below 80% of replacement cost AND below max NFIP limit
- 2-4 family dwellings
- Units not used exclusively as single-family dwelling
- Detached garages
- All personal property
- Appliances, carpets, carpet pads
- Outdoor awnings, antennas, outdoor equipment
- Abandoned property remaining as debris
- Non-principal-residence dwellings

**Under-insurance penalty**: If dwelling is insured below 80% and below NFIP max, you get the greater of: (a) ACV of damaged portion, or (b) a proportional share of replacement cost = (actual insurance / required insurance) x repair cost.

### 80% Calculation Exclusions

When calculating whether you meet the 80% threshold, do NOT include the value of: footings, foundations, piers, supports below lowest basement floor, below-ground supports inside foundation walls (if no basement), underground flues/pipes/wiring/drains. ICC coverage is also excluded from this calculation.

## Documentation Requirements

### Proof of Loss (Due Within 60 Days of Loss)

Signed and sworn statement providing:
- Date and time of loss
- Brief explanation of how the loss happened
- Your interest in the property (e.g., "owner") and interests of others
- Details of any other insurance covering the loss
- Changes in title or occupancy during policy term
- **Specifications of damaged buildings and detailed repair estimates**
- Names of mortgagees or lienholders
- Details of who occupied the building and for what purpose
- Inventory of damaged personal property

### Inventory of Damaged Property

Must show: quantity, description, actual cash value, and amount of loss. Attach all bills, receipts, and related documents.

### Additional Documentation We May Request

- Complete inventory with quantities, costs, ACV or replacement cost, amounts claimed
- Written plans and specifications for repair
- Evidence that prior flood damage has been repaired

### Adjuster's Report

We may accept the adjuster's report in lieu of your proof of loss. You must sign it (and may be required to swear to it). The adjuster is NOT authorized to approve or disapprove claims.

---

## Other Insurance Rules

- If other non-NFIP flood insurance exists, this policy pays proportionally
- If other policy declares itself excess, this policy is primary
- This policy is primary up to the other policy's deductible, then proportional
- If condo association has NFIP coverage on same property, this policy is excess (with exceptions for uninsured assessment losses)
- Combined NFIP coverage cannot exceed $250,000 per condo unit

---

## Pair and Set Rule

For loss to part of a pair or set, we pay either: (a) cost to replace the lost/damaged article minus depreciation, or (b) the fair proportion of total pair/set value that the article represents. Our option.

## Salvage

We may let you keep damaged property and reduce loss payment by salvage value.

## Continuous Lake Flooding

If building flooded by rising lake waters for 90+ continuous days and loss will equal or exceed policy limits, we may pay the full limit early -- but you must release all further claims, not renew, and not seek future NFIP coverage at that location.
