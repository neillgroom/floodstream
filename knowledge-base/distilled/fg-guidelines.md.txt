# Fountain Group Guidelines — Distilled

**Authority**: Lowest tier. Only cite when NFIP policy and claims manual do not cover the topic. These are FG internal procedures that supplement or differ from standard NFIP guidance.

---

## New Adjuster Pre-Review

- New-to-FG adjusters: FG reviews several completed claims before adjuster discusses estimate with policyholder. Scope can be reviewed during inspection, but hold the estimate until FG approves.
- **Progressive claims**: Company Pre-Review required on ALL claims before discussing estimate or obtaining signed POL. Only exception: large events where Progressive waives pre-review (FG notifies by email).

## Preliminary Reports

- Include policyholder phone number(s) and email address.
- Depth on prelim must match photos.
- Water depth photos must show visible water line, flood debris, or confirmation of actual depth.
- Photo #1 must include: FIRM status (pre/post), Elevated/Non-elevated/Basement, water depths (interior and exterior), duration of flood, flood zone.
- Cat Code (EDN) on prelim if applicable.
- Tape measurement photos required for both exterior and interior water lines. If no water line visible, photograph tape measure with pen/clothespin at estimated depth. Photo comments must explain estimation method.

### Private Flood/Excess Claims

- Do NOT put Cat Number (EDN) or FCN number on reports. Use State License Number.
- Use non-NFIP forms. Never mention NFIP/FEMA on Private Flood claims.

## Advances

- Advance and Prelim uploaded separately. Advance form package includes: Preliminary Report, FCN Card, Prelim Photos.
- If no advance requested, must explain WHY in prelim report (FCN Card or Photo 1 comments). "No advance requested" is not valid. Must state reason (prior loss, small loss with large deductible, PFE limitations, policyholder not present/declined).

### Advance Limits by Carrier

| Carrier | Advance Limit |
|---|---|
| **Progressive** | $10,000-$15,000 range (max 20% of reserves). Higher for total loss (case by case). |
| **Wright/National General/Liberty Mutual/Selective/NFIP Direct/Bankers/Tower Hill** | Up to 25% of reserve amounts (maximum). |
| **Bankers** | Must use their advance form (Venue app form also accepted). |

### Advance Cautions

- Prior loss/coverage issues: reduce advance or skip entirely.
- Contents advances: ensure contents loss exceeds advance after depreciation and deductible. Include photos of damaged contents.
- Prior loss + advance: must include short captioned status-style report stating that prior loss repairs were made (based on inspection or policyholder documents). If prior repairs not made, do not request advance.

## Status Reports

- **Required every 30 days** for all open claims including supplemental (RAP) claims. Clock starts on prelim submission or RAP opening.
- Update reserves on every status report. If estimate written and waiting on policyholder, update reserves. If estimate not written and original reserves still good, note that.
- Reserve amount = net amount expected to recommend (payment amounts). On RAP claims, only the net supplemental amount.

## Engineer Requests

- Request as soon as possible. Requests 30-60 days after inspection are a concern to FG and carriers.
- If policyholder claims foundation/structural damage, consider requesting during initial inspection.
- FG does not have authority to hire engineers — can only request that WYO/DSA make the request. Do NOT tell policyholder an engineer will be hired; say you will make the request.
- Do NOT tell policyholder to hire their own engineer.
- **Ceramic tile on slab**: All carriers want engineer or tile expert. Tile over subfloors does not require engineer if damage is evident. Progressive hires an engineer every time there is a tile dispute.

## Estimating — FG-Specific Requirements

### Debris Removal / Dumpsters

- If city/county picking up curbside debris: do NOT include dumpsters for that debris. DO include dumpsters for reconstruction debris with note: "Reconstruction Debris."
- If city/county NOT picking up, or dumpster on site/invoiced: include dumpsters for demo debris. Discuss in narrative.
- **Torrent Companies** (excluding Bankers, First Community, Selective): receipt required for dumpster.

### Sales Tax

- **Building**: Tax on Material and Equipment only, NOT on Labor/O&P (unless state requires tax on labor — document if so).
- **Contents**: Add sales tax to totals.
- **Adjuster invoice (Texas only)**: Add Texas Tax 8.25% as separate line item.

### Backer Board / Sheathing

- If submerged for hours/days, replacement may be warranted. Must show visible flood damage in photos.
- **Brick veneer**: Replace sheathing from interior (not exterior). If contractor says brick must be removed, discuss with FG management — may need engineer.
- Estimate line items must specify material type (plywood, OSB, gypsum). Use separate remove and replace line items in exterior section, not room by room.
- **Siding**: Consider removing and resetting siding rather than interior replacement.

### Drywall

- Remove/replace at 4' or 8' depending on water depth.
- Texture 1' above drywall cut line to blend new with old.
- If policyholder removed only 2', replace only 2'.
- If replacing 8' drywall, address ceiling paint and possibly ceiling texture (tape joint at ceiling/wall).

### Paint — FG Format

Two separate line items required:
1. **New drywall**: "Seal and Paint walls (two coats)" — total 3 coats (1 seal + 2 paint).
2. **Existing drywall**: "Paint Walls (one coat)" — one coat only.

Deduct ALL openings in all rooms. Must show on estimate and diagrams.

### Missing Walls / Cabinets / Tubs (Material Reduction)

- When calculating trim, paint, etc., deduct for cabinets, tubs, fireplaces, missing walls.

### Electrical

- Submerged receptacles/switches: include wire run replacement back to last connection (junction box) plus outlet/switch.
- Submerged breaker box: complete re-wire can be considered. Use correct pricing; do not include items (fixtures, etc.) not damaged by flood.

### A/C Units — FG Rule of Thumb

- Completely submerged: replacement without HVAC inspection (unless running at inspection).
- Partially submerged: if water over fan blades, recommend replacement. Below fan blades, require technician inspection.
- **Salt water claims**: consider replacement if >12" of water and/or water reaches electrical components.
- Determine unit size from model number (embedded number divisible by 12: 48=4 ton, 42=3.5 ton, 30=2.5 ton). Cross-check with ~1 ton per 500 SF of living area.
- Package units (combined air handler + condenser): identified by duct work connection.
- Only owe for direct physical loss by flood — do not pay for undamaged inside unit even if incompatible with new outside unit.

### Granite / Solid Surface Countertops

- Submerged: consider replacement. If policyholder saving/reusing, pay cleaning + removal/reset only.
- Not submerged: default to removal and reset. If broken during removal, need contractor written statement + photos.
- No longer available for inspection: policyholder must prove material type claimed.
- Contractor negligence (sledgehammer demo, broke everything): may not owe for avoidable damage.

### Tile Floors on Slab

- Must show evidence of physical change. Flood water generally does not damage tile; mastic is not water soluble. Hollow areas and cracks are typically pre-existing.
- Re-grouting is allowed. Do not add "Clean Tile Flooring" on top of re-grout (included in operation).

### Contents — FG Requirements

- Must verify: item was in risk, item was damaged by flood, and price on unusual/big-ticket items.
- Any item >$2,500 replacement cost: price verification required. Include supporting documentation.
- Bulk items must be broken out (no lump sums). Example: "Men's Clothes 1 @ $5,500" is NOT acceptable. Break into dress shirts, polos, jeans, etc.
- Photos must support claimed quantities. $50K contents estimate needs more than a handful of photos.
- Consider cleaning/refinishing/repair before replacement on every item.
- Contents age: flag claims with $75K+ replaced in past 24 months — explain how policyholder paid for this.

### Depreciation

- Take proper depreciation on every item individually. Do NOT use same percentage across all items.
- Avoid round numbers (10%, 20%, 50%) — looks arbitrary.
- Must explain reasoning, especially for lower-than-usual depreciation.
- Building valuation depreciation and estimate depreciation should be comparable (not 40% valuation vs. 10% estimate).

### Moisture / Mildew / Mold

- Do NOT include adjuster photos with moisture meters (mitigation company photos are OK).
- If risk sat in water for several days and mold rises above water line: can make allowances with good photos and detailed narrative. Treat as though wet — pay to remove and replace the item.
- Flood program pays for water mitigation, NOT mold remediation.

## APDA (Substantial Damage)

- Required when estimate exceeds 50% of estimated replacement cost.
- Completely destroyed/washed away: submit APDA immediately with prelim.
- Email APDA directly to FEMA. Include copy with prelim, status, or final report.
- APDA addresses full damage amount (not just flood). If risk substantially damaged by wind but minimal flood damage, APDA still required.
- One policyholder per APDA submission.

## Supplements (RAP) — FG Process

- **All RAPs must be reviewed by FG before POL sent to policyholder.**
- **Progressive**: Pre-review/approval required before sending POL. Do NOT send estimate copy to policyholder before Progressive approves. For price increase claims, submit status-style report with policyholder documentation before writing the RAP estimate. Progressive reviews first to determine if documentation suffices. Exception: omitted items do not need pre-review.
- **Wright & NFIP Direct**: Complete re-write of claim required on all RAPs.
- **All other clients**: Supplemental estimate only (unless multiple price changes + new items warrant full re-write — check with management).
- **Torrent**: Close RAP claims within 21 days unless policyholder fully cooperating with documentation requests. Does not apply to Bankers, First Community, Selective.
- Status reports due every 30 days on supplemental claims.
- POL does not require all named policyholders — just one signature.

## Closure Priority (FG Production Hierarchy)

Close smallest/least complicated claims first to maintain production numbers:
1. Erroneous claims
2. CWOP (closed without payment)
3. Estimates below deductible
4. Low limits claims
5. Claims well over policy limits
6. Claims with no contents coverage

Do not wait until fully inspected to start closing. Submit claims as completed — do not batch 10-12 at once.

## Driver's License / Principal Residence

| Carrier | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Progressive** | Driver's license on every claim. |
| **Wright Flood** | "Verification of Primary Residence" form — policyholder must choose "80% or more." |
| **All others** | Driver's license only when documenting principal residence. |

- License must be issued before DOL and not expired prior to DOL.
- If no license available: voter registration card, then homeowner's policy.
- **Principal vs. Primary**: Primary = 50%+ (underwriting). Principal = 80%+ (claims, required for replacement cost).

## Dry-Out Guidelines

Reference: FEMA bulletin W-13025a. Three methods based on policyholder's mitigation approach.

### Method 1 — SF Allowance (Policyholder Self-Drying)

- Used when policyholder does NOT use a mitigation company and does NOT rent equipment.
- Allowance covers: house fans, opening windows, running HVAC beyond regular use.
- Enter as single line item per total SF of affected area (room by room).
- Line item: "Structural Drying"
- Note: "Method 1 applied per FEMA bulletin W-13025a" — **do NOT add this note on Private Flood claims.**

### Method 2 — Equipment Allowance (No Drying Logs)

- Used when policyholder submits mitigation invoice but NO drying logs and/or NO itemized invoice.
- Equipment allowance based on risk size:
  - **Air movers**: 1 per 150-250 SF
  - **Dehumidifiers**: 1 per 350-500 SF
- **Maximum 3 days** allowed.
- Cannot allow for setup/takedown or monitoring under Method 2.

### Method 3 — Full Contractor Payment (With Drying Logs)

- Used when mitigation invoice submitted WITH appropriate drying logs.
- Can pay: contractor allowances for dry-out, tear-out, equipment setup, takedown, monitoring.
- Contractor costs must still be reasonable.
- **Prerequisite**: Wet building materials must be removed BEFORE dry-out starts. If not removed, contractor did not follow IICRC S500 industry standards — cannot use Method 3.

### Dry-Out Escalation Rules

- **Dry-out exceeding 5 days**: Discuss with FG management before proceeding.
- FEMA does not have drying guidelines but requires contractors follow IICRC S500 industry standards. Non-compliance = program does not owe for charges.

### Drying Log Requirements

A properly completed drying log must include:
- Daily temperature and relative humidity (indoor and outdoor)
- Moisture readings with recorded locations (affected and unaffected materials, each room)
- Drying goal and dry standard for affected materials
- Daily operating status of HVAC and all dehumidification equipment
- Name, model, and capacity of all dehumidification, air moving, and heating equipment
- Name, make, and model of moisture reading equipment
- Water category and water class type
- Cubic footage of affected area

**Flood claims are always Category 3 floodwater, Water Class 4.**

Moisture map not required but indicates quality work.

### Dehumidifier Sizing (Method 2)

- **LGR units**: Volume of affected area (cubic feet) / 50 = required AHAM pints
- **Desiccant units**: Volume of affected area (cubic feet) / 60 x 2 = required CFM
- When both types used in same area, combined capacity must not exceed single-formula capacity
- Standard/conventional refrigerant units should NOT be considered
- Unfinished basements/cellars: reduce dehumidification capacity
- Crawlspaces, attached garages, storage rooms exposed to soil/outdoors: no dehumidification
- Tolerance: if invoiced equipment within 10-15% of calculated amount, honor the difference. Beyond 15%, do not allow excess capacity.

### Dehumidifier Reference Sizes

| Type | Size | Capacity |
|---|---|---|
| LGR Standard | — | 70 PPD |
| LGR Large | — | 130 PPD |
| LGR Extra-Large | — | 170 PPD |
| Desiccant Standard | — | 385 CFM (116 PPD) |
| Desiccant Large | — | 900 CFM (344 PPD) |
| Desiccant Trailer (Standard) | — | 1,800 CFM (1,000 PPD) |
| Desiccant Trailer (Large) | — | 4,800 CFM (3,032 PPD) |

### Air Mover Guidelines (Method 2)

Per SF ratios based on total affected area:

| Total Affected SF | Scenario A/B | Scenario C | Scenario D |
|---|---|---|---|
| 600 SF | 1 per 60 SF | 1 per 65 SF | 1 per 200 SF |
| 1,200 SF | 1 per 75 SF | 1 per 90 SF | 1 per 250 SF |
| 1,800 SF | 1 per 85 SF | 1 per 106 SF | 1 per 300 SF |
| 2,400 SF | 1 per 92.5 SF | 1 per 124 SF | 1 per 350 SF |
| 3,000 SF | 1 per 100 SF | 1 per 140 SF | 1 per 400 SF |

**Drying Scenarios:**
- **A**: Floor coverings + trim removed. Dry structural floors, sill plates. At least 1 air mover per room/closet.
- **B**: A + perimeter walls + insulation removed. Dry A + perimeter sheathing + studs. 1 per 16 LF + 1 per room/closet.
- **C**: A+B + interior walls removed. Dry B + interior wall studs. 1 per 16 LF + 1 per 200-400 SF.
- **D**: A+B+C + sheathing removed. Dry A + all wall studs. 1 per 200-400 SF.

**Multi-day duration (surface, free, and bound water):**
- Day 2: 50% of Day 1 air movers
- Day 3: Sum of Day 2 + Day 3 equals Day 1

**Bound water only:** Same number of air movers each day.

If mechanical drying starts days after cleaning/sanitizing, increase per-SF amount up to +50%. If drying starts considerably later, refer entire invoice for independent review.

### Other Dry-Out Rules

- **Water extraction**: Pump-out (pump + hose + monitoring) is different cost than manual extraction (wet vacuum/specialized equipment). Extraction to aid carpet/padding removal is included in removal price. Extraction during cleanup included in cleanup price.
- **Other equipment**: Generators, negative air movers, air filters, PPE, and associated labor for demo/cleaning/mildewcide are included in those unit prices — not separately billable.
- **Sealers/preservatives**: Not allowed on items being dried unless documentation proves sealer was applied before the loss.
- **Temporary power**: Generator for drying when building electrical not operable is NOT allowed. Exception: generators for large desiccant dehumidifiers with validated capacity and proper drying log.
- **No drying before HVAC restoration**: If mechanical drying performed before tear-out, cleanup, sanitizing, and full HVAC restoration, all temporary climate control and subsequent cleaning/sanitizing/drying charges are NOT covered.
- **O&P on drying**: Only allowed when properly completed drying log is submitted and contractor hired/performed the services.
