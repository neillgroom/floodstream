# NFIP Standard Flood Insurance Policy — General Property Form

This form applies to non-residential buildings and non-residential condominiums. It does NOT cover residential condominium buildings in Regular Program communities. For condo unit owners, only personal property (Coverage B) is available under this form.

---

## Definition of Flood

A flood requires: a general and temporary condition of partial or complete inundation of **2+ acres** or **2+ properties** (at least one being the insured property) from:
- Overflow of inland or tidal waters
- Unusual and rapid accumulation or runoff of surface waters
- Mudflow (river of liquid mud — NOT landslide, slope failure, or saturated soil moving down a slope)
- Collapse or subsidence of land along a lakeshore from erosion/undermining by waves or currents exceeding cyclical levels

---

## Coverage A — Building Property

### What's Covered

- The described building on the Declarations Page
- Additions and extensions connected by rigid exterior wall, solid load-bearing interior wall, stairway, elevated walkway, or roof (may be insured separately at insured's option)
- Additions connected by a common interior wall that is NOT solid load-bearing — always part of the building, cannot be insured separately
- Building under construction, alteration, or repair at the described location
- Manufactured homes and qualifying travel trailers (must be anchored if in SFHA)
- Materials and supplies for construction/alteration/repair stored in a fully enclosed building at the described location or adjacent property

### Fixtures Covered Under Coverage A Only

Awnings, canopies, blinds, carpet permanently installed over unfinished flooring, central air conditioners, elevator equipment, fire extinguishing apparatus, fire sprinkler systems, walk-in freezers, furnaces, light fixtures, outdoor antennas attached to buildings, permanently installed cupboards/bookcases/paneling/wallpaper, pumps and pump machinery, ventilating equipment, permanently installed wall mirrors.

**In condo units (owned in common):** built-in dishwashers, built-in microwave ovens, garbage disposals, hot water heaters (including solar), kitchen cabinets, plumbing fixtures, radiators, ranges, refrigerators, stoves.

### Basement and Below-BFE Enclosure — Limited Coverage (Coverage A)

In basements (any zone) or enclosures below the lowest elevated floor of post-FIRM elevated buildings in A and V zones, Coverage A is limited to:

- Central air conditioners (installed, connected)
- Cisterns and water in them
- **Drywall**: walls and ceilings in basement only — limited to cost to nail it unfinished, unfloated, and not taped to framing
- Electrical junction/circuit breaker boxes, outlets, switches
- Elevators, dumbwaiters, related equipment (except equipment installed below BFE after 9/30/1987)
- Fuel tanks and fuel
- Furnaces and hot water heaters
- Heat pumps
- Nonflammable insulation (basement only)
- Pumps and tanks for solar energy systems
- Stairways/staircases attached to building (not separated by elevated walkways)
- Sump pumps
- Water softeners, chemicals, water filters, faucets integral to plumbing
- Well water tanks and pumps
- Required utility connections for listed items
- Footings, foundations, posts, pilings, piers, foundation walls, anchorage systems
- **Clean-up costs**

### Buildings Under Construction

- If not yet walled/roofed: covered only while work is in progress, or up to 90 continuous days after work halts
- NO coverage until walled and roofed if the lowest floor is below BFE in A or V zones

## Coverage B — Personal Property

Must be purchased separately. Covers direct physical loss by flood to personal property **inside a fully enclosed insured building**.

Coverage is either household personal property OR business personal property — not both under one policy.

### Household Personal Property

Covers property usual to living quarters belonging to the insured, household members, or (at insured's option) domestic workers or guests. Also covers property for which the insured may be legally liable.

### Business Personal Property

Covers furniture/fixtures, machinery/equipment, stock, and other business personal property owned by the insured.

### Items Covered Under Coverage B Only

Window/portable air conditioning units, carpet not permanently installed over unfinished flooring, carpet over finished flooring, clothes washers/dryers, cook-out grills, food freezers (not walk-in) and food in any freezer, outdoor equipment and furniture stored inside, ovens, portable microwave ovens, portable dishwashers.

### Basement and Below-BFE Enclosure — Limited Coverage (Coverage B)

In basements or below-BFE enclosures of post-FIRM elevated buildings, personal property coverage is limited to:
- Portable or window-type air conditioning units (installed, connected)
- Clothes washers and dryers (installed, connected)
- Food freezers (not walk-in) and food in any freezer (installed, connected)

### Special Limits — $2,500 Cap

Maximum $2,500 for any loss to one or more of:
- Artwork, photographs, collectibles, memorabilia (including porcelain figures, sports cards)
- Rare books or autographed items
- Jewelry, watches, precious/semiprecious stones, gold/silver/platinum articles
- Furs or articles where fur is the principal value

### Other Personal Property Rules

- **Antiques**: paid at functional value only (not collector value)
- **Tenants**: may apply up to 10% of Coverage B limit to improvements made to the building at tenant's expense (does not increase total Coverage B)
- **Condo unit owners**: may apply up to 10% of Coverage B limit to interior walls, floors, and ceilings not covered by the association's master policy (does not increase total Coverage B)
- **Tenant personal property**: must be inside the fully enclosed building
- **Property removed to safety**: covered for 45 days at temporary location

---

## Coverage C — Other Coverages

### Debris Removal

- Pays to remove non-owned debris on/in insured property and owned debris anywhere
- If insured performs removal, labor valued at federal minimum wage
- Does NOT increase Coverage A or B limits

### Loss Avoidance Measures

**Sandbags, Supplies, and Labor — up to $1,000**
- Sandbags and sand, fill for temporary levees, pumps, plastic sheeting and lumber
- Insured's labor at federal minimum wage
- Requires: flood damage imminent AND either (a) general flooding in the area even if it doesn't reach the building, or (b) legally authorized evacuation/civil order issued

**Property Removed to Safety — up to $1,000**
- Reasonable expenses to move insured property to protect from flood
- Includes insured's labor at federal minimum wage
- Property covered at temporary location for 45 consecutive days
- Moved property must be in a fully enclosed building or reasonably protected from elements
- Must be placed above ground level or outside the SFHA

Neither loss avoidance measure increases Coverage A or B limits. **No deductible** applies.

### Pollution Damage — up to $10,000

- Covers damage caused by pollutants to covered property if the discharge/seepage/release was caused by flood
- Does NOT cover testing for or monitoring pollutants unless required by law or ordinance
- Does not increase Coverage A or B limits
- Combined with all other payments for the same loss, cannot exceed RCV or ACV of covered property

---

## Coverage D — Increased Cost of Compliance (ICC)

### Limit: Up to $30,000

- Only available with building coverage (Coverage A)
- Paid in addition to Coverage A amount, but combined Coverage A + D cannot exceed statutory maximum
- **No separate deductible**

### What It Pays For

Cost to comply with state or local floodplain management laws after flood damage: elevation, floodproofing (nonresidential only, or residential with qualifying basements), relocation, or demolition.

### Eligibility — Must Meet One of Two Tests

1. **Repetitive loss**: 2 flood losses in 10 years, each averaging 25%+ of market value to repair, prior claim paid by NFIP, and state/community has repetitive loss provision being enforced
2. **Substantial damage**: single flood loss with repair cost at 50%+ of market value, and state/community has substantial damage provision being enforced

### ICC Exclusions

- Emergency Program communities
- Pollutant testing/cleanup/remediation costs
- Loss in building value due to ordinance requirements
- Residual value of undamaged portion of demolished building
- Not paid until compliance work is actually done
- Must complete compliance work within 2 years of loss
- Code upgrades not related to floodplain management (e.g., electrical, plumbing)
- Additions or improvements made after the loss
- Pre-existing compliance obligations
- Garages or carports
- Group Flood Insurance Policies
- Condo association assessments on individual unit owners

---

## Property NOT Covered (Exclusions from Coverage)

1. Personal property not inside the fully enclosed building
2. Buildings (and contents) located entirely in/on/over water or seaward of mean high tide, if built or substantially improved after 9/30/1982
3. Open structures, boathouses, structures into which boats are floated, personal property located in/on/over water
4. Recreational vehicles (except qualifying travel trailers on permanent foundations)
5. Self-propelled vehicles and machines — EXCEPT unlicensed vehicles used to service the location or designed for handicapped persons, while inside a building at the described location
6. Land, land values, lawns, trees, shrubs, plants, growing crops, animals
7. Accounts, bills, coins, currency, deeds, evidences of debt, medals, money, scrip, stored value cards, postage stamps, securities, bullion, manuscripts, valuable papers
8. Underground structures and equipment (wells, septic tanks, septic systems)
9. Walks, walkways, decks, driveways, patios, and other surfaces outside the building's perimeter exterior walls (whether roofed or not)
10. Containers and related equipment (e.g., tanks with gases or liquids)
11. Buildings or units where 49%+ of ACV is below ground (unless lowest level is at/above BFE and below ground only due to earth insulation for energy efficiency)
12. Fences, retaining walls, seawalls, bulkheads, wharves, piers, bridges, docks
13. Aircraft, watercraft, and their furnishings/equipment
14. Hot tubs and spas (not bathroom fixtures), swimming pools, and their equipment (heaters, filters, pumps, pipes) — wherever located
15. Property barred under Coastal Barrier Resources Act / Coastal Barrier Improvement Act
16. Personal property owned by or in care/custody/control of a condo unit owner (except as covered under Coverage B)
17. Residential condominium building in a Regular Program community

## Key Exclusions (Causes of Loss Not Covered)

### Never Covered

- Loss of revenue, profits, access, use, or business interruption
- Additional living expenses during repairs
- Code compliance costs (except eligible ICC activities under Coverage D)
- Any other economic loss
- Flood already in progress when policy begins or coverage is added

### Earth Movement — Not Covered Even If Caused by Flood

Earthquake, landslide, land subsidence, sinkholes, destabilization from subsurface water accumulation, gradual erosion. **Exception**: mudflow and lakeshore subsidence from erosion ARE covered under the flood definition.

### Other Excluded Causes

- Pressure or weight of ice
- Freezing or thawing
- Rain, snow, sleet, hail, water spray
- Water/moisture/mildew/mold damage that is substantially confined to the building or within the insured's control (design defects, plumbing failure, failure to inspect/maintain after flood recedes)
- Sewer/drain backup, sump pump overflow, seepage — UNLESS a flood in the area is the proximate cause
- Pressure or weight of water — UNLESS a flood in the area is the proximate cause
- Power/heating/cooling failure — UNLESS caused by direct flood damage to equipment at the described location
- Theft, fire, explosion, wind, windstorm
- Deliberate acts by the insured
- Alteration of property that significantly increases flood risk

## Deductibles

- Separate deductibles apply to building and personal property for each flood loss
- Deductible amount shown on Declarations Page
- **Building under construction** (not yet walled/roofed): deductible is **2x** the normal amount
- **No deductible** on Loss Avoidance Measures or ICC (Coverage D)

---

## Loss Settlement / Valuation

Payment is the **least** of (after deductible):
1. The applicable policy limit
2. The actual cash value (replacement cost minus depreciation)
3. The cost to repair or replace with material of like kind and quality within a reasonable time

**This is NOT a valued policy.** The insured and insurer do not pre-agree on property value.

### Pairs and Sets

Insurer may pay either:
- Cost to replace the damaged article less depreciation, OR
- Fair proportion of total value of the pair/set

### Salvage

Insurer may let the insured keep damaged property and reduce the loss payment by salvage value.

### Appraisal Process

If ACV is disputed, either party may demand appraisal. Each chooses an appraiser within 20 days. The two appraisers choose an umpire (court-appointed if they can't agree within 15 days). Agreement by any two of the three sets the loss amount. Each side pays its own appraiser; umpire costs split equally.

---

## Documentation Requirements — What the Insured Must Do After a Loss

1. **Prompt written notice** to insurer
2. **Separate damaged from undamaged property** as soon as reasonably possible, organize for examination
3. **Inventory of damaged property**: quantity, description, actual cash value, amount of loss — attach all bills, receipts, related documents
4. **Proof of loss within 60 days** — signed, sworn statement including:
   - Date and time of loss
   - How the loss happened
   - Insured's interest (owner, etc.) and interests of others
   - Other insurance details
   - Changes in title or occupancy during policy term
   - **Specifications of damaged buildings and detailed repair estimates**
   - Names of mortgagees or lienholders
   - Building occupancy at time of loss and purpose
   - The inventory of damaged property
5. Insured must use own judgment on loss amount and justify it
6. Must cooperate with adjuster's investigation
7. Adjuster may help with proof of loss form as a courtesy, but the 60-day deadline applies regardless

### What the Insurer May Request

- Complete inventory with quantities, costs, ACV, amounts claimed
- Written plans and specifications for repair
- Evidence that prior flood damage has been repaired
- Examination under oath
- Access to insurance policies, deeds, condo documents, books of accounts, bills, invoices

### Loss Payment Timing

Payable 60 days after proof of loss received (or 90 days after adjuster's report filed in lieu of proof of loss), once agreement is reached, final judgment entered, or appraisal award filed.

### Amended Proof of Loss

If the claim is rejected in whole or in part, the insured may file an amended proof of loss within 60 days of the date of loss.

## Other Rules That Affect Coverage Decisions

### Manufactured Homes in SFHA

Must be anchored by over-the-top or frame ties, per manufacturer specs, or per community requirements — unless continuously NFIP-insured at the same location since 9/30/1982.

### Continuous Lake Flooding

If a building has been flooded by rising lake waters continuously for 90+ days and total loss appears certain, insurer will pay building limits + deductible (or max payable) if the insured agrees to: make no further claims, not renew, not seek new NFIP coverage at that location, and not seek premium refunds.

### Other Insurance

If other non-NFIP flood insurance exists, NFIP pays its proportionate share. NFIP is primary up to the other policy's deductible, then proportionate. For condominiums, the association policy is primary over unit owner policies.

### Hazard Increase

No coverage for loss occurring while there is a hazard increased by any means within the insured's control or knowledge.
