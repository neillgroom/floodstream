# NFIP Claims Manual 2025 — Distilled

Source: NFIP Claims Manual, March 2025 Edition. This is an aggressive distillation for automation reference — not a substitute for the full manual.

---

## Authority Hierarchy

When citing rules or answering questions, follow this order of authority:

1. **SFIP Policy Forms** (Dwelling, General Property, RCBAP) — the binding contract. Highest authority.
2. **NFIP Claims Manual** — FEMA's official interpretation and procedures. Second authority.
3. **FEMA Commentary** — interpretive rulings on ambiguous policy language.
4. **Fountain Group / Adjusting Company Guidelines** — internal procedures. Cite ONLY when no higher authority covers the topic.

## Role & Discretion — CRITICAL

- Adjusters **recommend**. The adjusting company can overrule any recommendation before it reaches the carrier. The **carrier has final say** on all coverage decisions, payments, and interpretations.
- Adjusters have **ZERO discretion** over the rules, their enforcement, or whether they can be bent. Documentation standards are non-negotiable — unacceptable documentation cannot be accepted regardless of circumstances.
- We will submit anything that is in the correct format. We can share what we think the carrier will likely think about it, but the decision is not ours.
- On pricing: we can advise whether a price deviation looks reasonable and well-documented enough that the carrier is likely to accept it. But once a price is submitted and rejected, coming back with a lower number damages credibility and makes acceptance harder. It is in everyone's interest to submit reasonable, documented pricing the first time.

## 1. Cause of Loss Determination

### Flood Definition (SFIP II.B)
- Requires partial or complete inundation of **two or more acres** of normally dry land, OR **two or more parcels** (one can be public property like a road or park).
- Acres must be continuous; parcels must touch.
- Includes mudflow and collapse/subsidence of land from erosion.

### Confirming Flood Occurred
- Adjuster must confirm a flood as defined by SFIP Article II.B occurred at or near the described location.
- Must confirm flood was the **proximate cause** of damage to insured property.
- May require neighborhood canvass, weather bureau searches, local news/social media, contacting police/fire.
- Must document: when flooding began, when it receded, source of water, whether flood caused direct physical loss.

### Documenting No Condition of Flood
Claim file must include:
- Photos showing lack of exterior waterline at lowest adjacent grade
- Lack of flood-borne debris in yard; undisturbed vegetation
- Interview notes from neighbors

### Direct Physical Loss by or from Flood (SFIP II.C.14)
- Flood must be the **immediate cause** — no intervening cause or factor.
- Must show visible evidence flood ruined, damaged, or changed the property.
- Expressly covered even without direct contact: sewer/drain backup where flood is proximate cause; sump pump overflow where flood is proximate cause; seepage where flood is proximate cause; hydrostatic pressure from flood.

### Flood-in-Progress Exclusion (SFIP V.B)
- SFIP will NOT pay for damage from a flood that was a continuation of a flood existing:
  - Prior to coverage becoming effective, OR
  - On or before the day the application and premium were submitted
- Applied regardless of property, city, county, or parish boundaries.
- NOT triggered only when floodwaters physically touch the building.
- Evidence includes: flood in area from same source; spillway opening; levee breach; dam release; water escaping banks.
- Each flood-in-progress must be addressed separately; there may be more than one.

### Multiple Perils
- SFIP is a **single-peril policy** — pays only for direct physical loss by or from flood.
- When other perils involved, adjuster must separate flood scope from other peril scope.
- If scopes overlap or border each other, flood adjuster must contact other adjuster to confirm extents.
- If combined repair estimates exceed building value = total loss; pay building valuation less other peril scope, depreciation, and deductible.
- Other Insurance provision (VII.B) may require proportional distribution.

### Earth Movement Exclusion
- SFIP does NOT cover earth movement — each form is an intervening cause and separate peril.
- Does NOT cover damage from saturated soils causing sinking, expanding, compacting, destabilizing, or losing load-bearing capacity.
- DOES cover collapse/subsidence from **sudden erosion** or undermining of building support soil by waves/currents of floodwater (velocity flow) or mudflow. Gradual erosion is NOT covered.

## 2. Coverage Rules and Interpretations

### SFIP Analytical Hierarchy
1. **Eligibility** — Whether elements for a valid claim exist
2. **Coverage** — Whether the property is covered
3. **Scope** — Determining extent of covered damage
4. **Valuation** — How to determine the amount of loss

### Three SFIP Forms
| Form | Policyholders | Building Types |
|------|--------------|----------------|
| **Dwelling** | Homeowners, residential unit owners, renters | 1-4 family, manufactured homes, residential units |
| **General Property** | Building/unit owners, lessees | 5+ family, non-residential, commercial, detached garages |
| **RCBAP** | Condo associations | Residential condo buildings (75%+ residential) |

### Coverage A — Building Property

**Additions/Extensions**: Must connect via one of "REELS" — Roof, Elevated walkways, Exterior rigid walls, Load-bearing interior walls, Stairs. Must be permanently attached. Canvas, tarp, netting, umbrellas do NOT qualify.

**Detached Garages (Dwelling only)**: Up to 10% of dwelling coverage. Must have garage door/opening for automobile. Cannot be used for residential, business, or farming purposes.

**Building Under Construction**: Must have at least 2 rigid exterior walls and fully secured roof at time of loss. If not walled/roofed, coverage only while work is in progress, up to 90 continuous days if halted. Deductible doubles if lacking walls/roof at time of loss.

**Items Only Under Building Coverage (III.A.7)**: Includes blinds (not drapes), HVAC components, walk-in equipment (must be permanently installed/built-in), central A/C, ranges/cooktops/ovens, refrigerators (including beverage coolers), wine refrigerators.

**Coverage Limitations — Basements (III.A.8)**:
- Basement = below ground level on **all sides**
- Sunken rooms count as basements if floor is below ground on all sides
- Limited coverage: only specifically listed items (HVAC, electrical panels, water heaters, pumps, etc.)
- Unfinished/unfloated/untaped drywall IS covered in basements
- Clean-up of covered items IS covered
- Finished items (flooring, lighting, cabinets, etc.) are NOT covered in basements

**Coverage Limitations — Post-FIRM Elevated Buildings**:
- Apply in Zones A1-A30, AE, AH, AR, AR/A, AR/AE, AR/AH, AR/A1-A30, V1-V30, VE
- Do NOT apply in Zones A, AO, A99, AR/AO, V, VO
- Full coverage begins at **lowest elevated floor** — the lowest floor raised above ground
- Items originating below/at/level with lowest elevated floor are NOT insured
- Exception: building materials extending above lowest elevated floor are covered above that line (siding, drywall, trim, paint, insulation) even if they extend below

**HVAC Coverage**:
- Condenser and heat pumps covered even in the open, if servicing the insured building
- SFIP does NOT pay to upgrade undamaged HVAC components due to SEER/refrigerant changes
- Pair and Set does NOT apply to HVAC systems
- Retrofitting undamaged components for compatibility is NOT covered

**Elevators**: Covered if directly attached to building or 16 SF landing at building door. One form of egress per side (stairs, ramp, or elevator). Elevators separated from building (e.g., parking lot to sidewalk) are excluded.

**Egress**: One 16 SF landing per egress point, single set of stairs per staircase. Materials of like kind and quality. Includes hand/support rails and posts. Does NOT include lighting or finishing.

### Coverage B — Personal Property
- Must be inside a building at described location (Dwelling) or inside insured building (GP/RCBAP)
- Property removed to safety: covered for **45 consecutive days** from date of removal
- Washers and dryers are ALWAYS personal property
- Refrigerators are ALWAYS building property
- **$2,500 special limit** on aggregate ACV of: jewelry, furs, contents used in business or held as stock

**Tenant Improvements**: Up to 10% of contents coverage limit for fixtures/alterations at tenant's expense. This is at policyholder's option and reduces personal property limit.

### Coverage C — Other Coverages

**Debris Removal**: Non-owned debris on/in insured property AND debris of insured property anywhere. Does NOT pay for removal of non-insured items owned by policyholder.

**Loss Avoidance**: Only specifically listed items. No coverage for permanently installed protective measures. Receipts required. Water-filled bladders count as temporary levees.

**Property Removed to Safety**: Covered for 45 consecutive days. Property must be moved FROM described location (not to upstairs/garage at same location). Up to $1,000 for costs of removal when imminent threat of flood.

**Contents Manipulation (Dwelling Form only)**: Moving undamaged personal property to facilitate building repairs. Must have both Coverage A and B. Does NOT include: moving damaged/non-covered contents, moving tenant-owned contents, lump-sum allowances without documentation, storage outside insured building or appurtenant building at described location.

### Property Not Insured (SFIP IV)
- Land, lawns, trees, shrubs, plants, animals
- Underground structures and equipment (but alarm panels above ground are covered)
- Walks, walkways, decks, driveways, patios (except 16 SF landing at egress)
- Fences, retaining walls, seawalls, bulkheads (unless integral to foundation)
- Self-propelled vehicles (except disability-assist vehicles inside building)
- Valuable papers, scrip, electronic data
- Aircraft, watercraft (recreational RC boats/drones are covered)
- Buildings entirely over water (constructed/improved after 9/30/1982)
- Recreational vehicles and motor homes

### Exclusions (SFIP V)
- Economic loss (packing, moving, storage costs; code compliance for undamaged property)
- Power/heating/cooling failure originating off-site (even if caused by flood)
- Alteration of property that significantly increases flood risk
- Pre-existing damage, rot, insect infestation, long-term moisture exposure

**Pollutants**: Dwelling/RCBAP — no sublimit. GP — $10,000 sublimit. Testing/monitoring only covered if required by law (copy of law must be in file). Mold is an indoor air pollutant per EPA definition. Abatement costs must be incurred and documented.

### Pair and Set (SFIP VII.A)
- If single item cannot be replaced individually and no functional value remains for undamaged item, SFIP may pay for pair/set.
- Applies: single shoe ruined, base of china cabinet ruined, one section of sectional sofa.
- Does NOT apply: upper cabinets to match damaged lower cabinets; HVAC indoor/outdoor units; where replacement with like kind and quality makes other items usable.

## 3. Estimate Requirements — CRITICAL

### Line-by-Line, Room-by-Room, Unit-Cost Basis
- **Estimates MUST be written line-by-line, room-by-room, using unit costs.**
- NFIP will NOT accept non-itemized, lump-sum, or single-line estimates or invoices.
- General contractor estimates that are not itemized room-by-room cannot be the **sole** basis for a claim payment.
- When contractor provides lump-sum, adjuster must break it down into individual components and price each one.

### Why Lump Sums Are Rejected
- Can mask inclusion of undamaged components
- Can hide betterments or code-required upgrades
- Can lead to pricing inaccuracy
- Can include non-covered items

### Unit Pricing
- Software must be calibrated for geographic area (city, county, or zip code).
- Room dimensions, floor SF, perimeter LF, and wall-to-ceiling SF must be shown.
- Missing walls and wall openings must be accounted for.
- Avoid non-descriptive (generic) line items — select most descriptive option available.
- Example: "Remove and replace drywall" is non-descriptive. Use "Remove and replace 1/2" residential wall drywall" or "Remove and replace 5/8" residential ceiling drywall."
- Any unit price adjustment requires a note explaining/justifying it.

### Labor Minimums and Minimum Charges
- When total repair for a trade doesn't meet minimum threshold, a "labor minimum" is acceptable.
- Related trades can be combined to satisfy the threshold (e.g., cleaning + pressure washing).
- A minimum charge for a trade represents the **maximum** for that trade — cannot add both a minimum charge AND line items for the same trade.
- Minimum charge allowances should be depreciated.

### Lump-Sum Allowances — Prohibited
- A lump-sum allowance for a larger item (e.g., "replace furnace: $12,000") must be broken into components.
- Example: A $12,000 furnace lump-sum could hide: A/C e-coil ($4,500), power exhaust ($600), dehumidifier ($500) — all non-covered items. Actual covered cost may be $5,500.

### Depreciation Requirements
- Apply depreciation based on **age and physical condition** of each item.
- Applying the same rate to all items (lump-sum depreciation) is **NOT acceptable**.
- Depreciation applies to material, labor, equipment, overhead, profit, and sales tax on all replacement items.
- Document support for depreciation rate or lack thereof.
- Consider previous repairs/modifications that extended item life.

### Overhead and Profit
- OHP typically 10% overhead + 10% profit when complexity requires general contractor coordination.
- Policyholder performing GC duties: up to 10% overhead only (no profit).
- **Non-OHP trades** (not included in OHP calculation): cleanup, mold treatment, building dry-out, HVAC, kitchen appliances, carpet/padding, contractor receipts/quotes, dumpsters.
- Specialty equipment (cranes, scaffolding, etc.) should be separate line items with invoice/estimate, not included in OHP.

### Sales Tax
- Adjuster MUST consistently document all state and local sales tax on applicable goods/services, subject to depreciation.

## 4. Documentation Requirements

### Photographs — Minimum Standards
- Take as many as necessary to support claim recommendation.
- Must photograph: evidence of flood/no flood, policy underwriting factors, covered scope of damage, non-covered damage from other perils.
- Properly annotate every photograph with description.
- Exterior photos must include location description (e.g., "north side," "SW corner," "rear crawlspace").

### Waterline Documentation — Critical
- A waterline is usually evidenced by flood debris on wall from grade/slab to water limit.
- Record highest exterior waterline at lowest adjacent grade (LAG).
- Photograph full height from ground to top of waterline.
- Take multiple interior waterlines — more documentation is better than less.
- If multiple floors, measurements for each level (basement, main floor, garage, etc.).
- **Missing or unclear waterline documentation makes it difficult or impossible to support that a flood occurred or validate scope of loss. Can call into question entire adjustment.**

### What Constitutes Acceptable Documentation
- Copies of canceled checks (front and back), credit card payments, paid contractor invoices, material purchase receipts.
- Contractor-signed repair agreements with corresponding itemized estimates.
- Photographs of damages (quantity, quality, extent).
- Serial numbers for appliances and equipment.
- Samples/swatches of flooring, wallpaper, upholstery.

### Unacceptable Documentation
- Non-itemized lump-sum or single-line estimates/invoices — **NFIP will not accept**.
- Unsigned estimate or proposal — not sufficient basis for a Request for Additional Payment.
- A RAP based on increases in unit pricing without proper documentation or justification is **not substantiated**.

### Prior Loss Documentation
- SFIP does NOT cover unrepaired damage from prior floods.
- If prior loss paid full replacement cost, NFIP will NOT pay again unless item was actually replaced (documented).
- Evidence of prior repairs: paid contractor invoices, receipts, comparison of prior/current claim photos.
- When documentation unavailable, visual observation of recent repairs can be documented in photos.
- When items were cleaned/repaired (not replaced) after prior loss, current loss pays for clean/repair only.

### Proof of Loss Requirements
- 60-day deadline from date of loss (FEMA may extend via bulletin).
- Must be signed under penalty of perjury by policyholder (or executor if deceased).
- Electronic signatures are acceptable.
- POL is not the claim — policyholder has one claim per flood, may submit multiple POLs.
- POL does not need to be notarized.
- If POL is untimely, insurer must request FEMA waiver through Pivot before paying.
- Insurer may accept signed adjuster's report instead of POL (at insurer's option).

## 5. Pricing Rules

### Database Pricing
- Estimating software unit prices are based on averages from past settled claims, national/regional pricing studies, or actual contractor estimates.
- Prices are "reasonable and customary to the loss and location" — average quantity, quality, workmanship, under average conditions.
- Software must be calibrated for loss location.

### Price Adjustments
- Database pricing is the default. **All estimates must use database pricing unless a documented deviation is justified.**
- When unit price adjustment is needed, include a note under the line item explaining/justifying the adjustment.
- Acceptable factors for deviation: post-disaster pricing (with market evidence), exceptional quantities, specialized craftsmanship, unusual assemblies.
- **Acceptable documentation for deviations**: paid invoices from the specific job, supplier price quotes with dates, published post-disaster pricing surveys, comparable completed-job documentation from the same market/timeframe.
- **NOT acceptable as justification**: verbal assertions that "prices went up," generic contractor statements without itemized backup, estimates from contractors who haven't visited the property, undated or unsigned quotes, lump-sum invoices, assertions about material quality without specifications.
- Any deviation submitted without acceptable documentation will not be considered. The carrier makes the final determination on whether a deviation is justified.
- **Pricing deviation risk**: Database pricing is the baseline (e.g., if the system says $1.66/SF for a task, that's the starting point). We don't know the carrier's exact threshold for rejection — it could be $4, it could be $7. But if a price is submitted and the carrier rejects it, it becomes very difficult to come back with a lower number and maintain credibility. A reasonable, well-documented deviation is far more likely to be accepted than an aggressive one. Submissions should reflect actual, documented costs — not aspirational pricing.

### Contractor Estimates
- Contractor estimates must be itemized line-by-line.
- If contractor provides lump-sum, adjuster should request detailed line-by-line estimate. **A lump-sum contractor estimate cannot be submitted — it will be rejected.**
- Request copy of contractor-signed repair agreement corresponding to estimate.
- Verify estimates are for like kind and quality, don't include undamaged property, and are reasonable/customary.
- If a contractor's line-item pricing significantly exceeds database pricing, the deviation must be documented and justified per the price adjustment rules above.

### Repair Receipts for Utilities
- Request itemized repair estimates for HVAC, electrical, plumbing.
- Record make, model, serial number of claimed equipment (reveals quality, size, capacity, date of manufacture).

### Building Tear-Out Pricing
- Use pricing charged by professional service if reasonable and customary.
- Peak pricing occurs first weeks after event; price should decline as more services become available.
- Document market conditions if justifying exceptionally priced invoice.

## 6. Structural Drying Rules

### Three Methods

**Method 1 — Square-Foot (policyholder self-dries)**: SF-based allowance for HVAC usage, policyholder labor, electricity, common household appliances. Different unit prices for floor-only vs. wall/subfloor removal; climate-controlled vs. non-climate-controlled.

**Method 2 — Adjuster-Determined (no drying log or improper log)**: When invoice lacks properly completed drying log per ANSI/IICRC S500-2021. Adjuster determines type, quantity, and duration of equipment. **Maximum 72 hours.** Use this method even with thorough-looking log if: insufficient dehumidification capacity, only carpet dryers above floor level, no declining air mover use, no heaters during final drying stage.

**Method 3 — Invoice with Proper Drying Log**: When drying follows industry standard. Technician enters required data before starting and at least every 24 hours. Can use invoiced charges including containment, ducting, heaters, labor, monitoring, overhead and profit. Standard drying completes in **less than 72 hours**. If exceeding 3 days, log must specify reason with supporting evidence — otherwise revert to Method 2.

### Drying Log Requirements (Properly Completed)
- Daily indoor/outdoor temperature and relative humidity
- Moisture readings of affected and unaffected materials (unaffected = dry goal)
- Daily temperature/humidity from HVAC and all dehumidifiers
- Make, model, capacity of all dehumidifiers each day
- Make, model, capacity of all supplemental heaters each day
- Type and number of air movers each day
- Cubic volume of affected air space
- Drying approach = Water Class 4
- Log that skips a day of monitoring = improper → Method 2

### Crawlspace Drying
- Vented crawlspaces with minor inundation often dry themselves.
- Dehumidification is NOT effective in crawlspaces (exposed to outside air, no climate control).
- If mechanical drying needed: single air mover + heater at each vent on windward side only. Max 72 hours.

### Drying Wet Concrete
- Concrete in contact with soil will not dry until moisture equilibrium with soil — can take very long time.
- Prolonged drying of concrete in contact with soil is against industry standard.
- After damage removal, cleaning, treating, and 72-hour drying: leave concrete to dry on its own.
- Prolonged wetness of concrete is NOT direct physical loss by flood — not covered.

## 7. Loss Settlement Procedures

### Types of Loss Settlement

**Replacement Cost (Dwelling, RCBAP)**: Must be single-family residence, principal residence, insured to at least 80% of full replacement cost OR maximum coverage available. Insurer does not have to withhold recoverable depreciation.

**Actual Cash Value**: Cost to repair/replace less depreciation and deductible.

**Proportional Loss (Dwelling only)**: When principal residence but doesn't meet 80% threshold. Formula: (Insurance carried / Insurance required) x RCV loss - deductible. Pay greater of proportional loss or ACV.

**Special Loss (Manufactured Homes)**: Total loss of manufactured home at least 16' wide, 600 SF, principal residence. Pay lesser of: RCV building valuation less deductible; 1.5x ACV building valuation less deductible; policy limits.

### Payment Limits
- Cannot exceed value of insured building before loss
- Limited to lesser of: coverage limits; replacement cost with like kind and quality; necessary amount spent to repair/replace
- Dwelling max: $250,000 building / $100,000 contents
- Commercial max: $500,000 building / $500,000 contents
- RCBAP max: $250,000 per unit

### Deductibles
- Separate deductibles for building and personal property.
- Subtract deductible from gross loss BEFORE applying policy limits.
- Building under construction without 2 rigid walls + secured roof: deductible **doubles**.
- GFIP: special $200 deductible for building and contents each.

### Other Insurance Formula
- NFIP share = (SFIP limit / total insurance) x (loss - other insurance deductible)
- NFIP payment = NFIP share + other insurance deductible - SFIP deductible

### Coinsurance (RCBAP only)
- Building must be insured to 80% of RCV or max available, whichever is less.
- If underinsured: (Coverage A / Required amount) x RCCL = Insurer's share
- Policyholder's share = RCCL - Insurer's share

## 8. Advance Payment Rules and Limits

### Pre-Inspection Advances (When Authorized by FEMA)

**Option 1 — $5,000**: Upon notice of loss. Building + contents combined, less deductibles. Requires: verbal/written statement of flood, source, description, supporting weather/news docs, approximate depth, brief description of damage, prior loss info.

**Option 2 — $20,000**: All Option 1 requirements PLUS photographs of depths/damage AND verification of out-of-pocket expenses (receipts, invoices).

### Post-Inspection Advances (When Authorized by FEMA)

**Option 1 — 25% Advance**: Up to 25% of reserve on Preliminary Report for each coverage type, less deductibles. Requires: Preliminary Report, photos, signed Advance Payment Request form, underwriting memoranda, APDA, prior loss narrative.

**Option 2 — 50% Advance**: Building coverage only. Up to 50% of reserve on Preliminary Report, less deductible. All Option 1 docs PLUS signed contractor contract with itemized repair estimate.

**Option 3 — BVLA**: Based on FEMA-authorized Building Valuation Loss Assessment. Requires properly completed BVLA worksheet (issued by FEMA during major events).

**Option 4 — WYO Proprietary**: Insurer's own approach. Cannot broaden or change SFIP terms. Must document deviations.

### Advance Payment Conditions
1. Not for non-SFIP expenses (evacuation, temporary housing, rental cars).
2. Does not prejudice or waive rights of either party.
3. Does not constitute admission of coverage.
4. Policyholder must assert insured loss.
5. If not insured loss or advance exceeds insured loss, policyholder must repay excess.
6. Does not affect right to seek additional payment.
7. Final payment reduced by advance amount(s).
8. Building advances must include mortgagee as co-payee.
9. Policyholder must execute POL for all amounts received.

### Avoiding Overpayment
Consider: deductibles, interior water depth, non-flood damage, pre-loss condition/ACV, scope limited to basement/enclosure, flood avoidance measures used.

## 9. ICC (Increased Cost of Compliance) Coverage

*Note: ICC applies in <1% of claims. Included for reference only.*

- Coverage D of SFIP. Up to **$30,000** toward bringing flood-damaged structure into compliance with floodplain management regulations.
- Requires community to declare building **substantially damaged** (typically 50%+ of market value).
- Eligible work: elevation, demolition, relocation, floodproofing.
- NOT available for: contents-only policies, condo unit policies, GFIP.
- Line-item estimates required (no lump sums). Refer to full Claims Manual Section 3 for detailed ICC procedures.

### Second-Story Conversion
- Alternative method when physically raising building is not feasible/cost-effective.
- Abandons lower first-floor interior, removes roof, retrofits foundation for code-compliant second story.
- Must meet SFIP definition of elevated building after reconstruction.
- Requires certification from registered design professional.

## 10. Manufactured/Mobile Home Specific Rules

### Eligibility
- Must be correctly affixed to a **permanent foundation** — look for chassis frame underneath.
- "Affixed by weight" does NOT mean affixed to permanent site.
- Travel trailers must have **all wheels removed**.
- Must be installed in compliance with floodplain management regulations.
- In SFHA, must have anchoring devices (over-the-top straps or tie-downs to chassis).
- Mobile homes = built before June 15, 1976. Manufactured homes = after (HUD standards).
- Modular homes are NOT manufactured homes (regulated by local/state building codes).

### Data Plates
- Located inside building: wall near main electric panel, inside utility closet door, master bedroom closet door, or under kitchen sink.
- Contains: manufacturer name, make, model, serial number, size, year of manufacture, code improvements.
- Travel trailers may have metal tag on chassis frame instead.
- If no data plate/tag: obtain property title or property card + bill of sale, manufacturer's build sheet, or homeowners insurance appraisal.

### Building Valuation
- Appraisal of factory-built unit alone is NOT sufficient.
- Complete valuation = appraisal + site-built additions + improved foundations + modifications not in appraisal.
- Must include both RCV and ACV.
- RCV appraisal includes: same/like kind factory-built unit with all improvements, sales tax, delivery.
- Include in valuation: disconnect/reconnect utilities, remove damaged unit, transportation, setup on existing foundation with tie-downs.
- Exclude from valuation: code compliance, upgrades, relocation to new foundation.

### Loss Settlement
- **Special Loss**: Total loss, 16'+ wide full length, 600+ SF, principal residence → pay lesser of: RCV less deductible; 1.5x ACV less deductible; policy limits.
- **Replacement Cost**: Not total loss, 16'+ wide, 600+ SF, principal residence, insured to 80% RCV → pay RCV estimate less non-recoverable depreciation and deductible.
- **ACV**: When above criteria not met → pay greater of ACV loss or proportional loss.

### Manufactured Home Estimating
- Select manufactured home specific pricing schedule in estimating software.
- Materials differ from site-built (door dimensions, wallboard thickness, trim, sheathing, subfloor).
- If specific line item unavailable, adjust unit price and document with notation.
- If site-built additions exceed original SF area, submit underwriting referral.

## 11. Subrogation Rules

- Subrogation = NFIP's right to recover costs from a third party that caused or contributed to the loss.
- If adjuster suspects third-party involvement: complete **Adjuster's Subrogation Referral** form.
- Claim handling and payment proceed as normal (do not delay).
- Insurer must notify FEMA within **10 calendar days** of identifying potential subrogation.
- Insurer must use customary business practices when pursuing subrogation.
- May refer subrogation claims to FEMA instead of pursuing directly.
- Send documentation to: FIMA-OCC-Subrogation@fema.dhs.gov, cc: NFIPClaimsMailbox@fema.dhs.gov.

## 13. Request for Additional Payment (RAP)

- RAP is NOT a new claim — policyholder has one claim per flood loss.
- NFIP may NOT deny RAP solely because repairs aren't completed or money isn't exhausted.
- Policyholder must prove their loss (same burden as initial settlement).

### RAP for Omission
- If item was omitted from original estimate and photos support it: notate file, get POL, pay.
- If quality was underestimated (e.g., hollow core door paid, actually solid core): notate, get POL, pay.
- If only difference is price (same quality, different amount): policyholder must prove actual repairs.

### RAP for Previously Included Items
- Must document remaining damage (if any) AND all repairs.
- Repaired damages: contractor estimate + proof of payment (canceled checks, credit card invoices, debit entries), paid receipts, photos.
- Unrepaired damages: signed contractor repair agreement with corresponding estimate, material purchase orders, photos. **Unsigned estimate is NOT sufficient.**
- Pricing RAP: additional payment only if documented loss and repair exceeds sum of all building payments + deductible + withheld depreciation.

## 14. Denial and Appeals

### Denial Letter Requirements
1. Date of denial letter (starts 60-day appeal period and 1-year suit period)
2. Policyholder name(s), mailing address, property location
3. Date of loss
4. Date(s) policyholder submitted payment request
5. Items denied with dollar amounts — NOT "various items" or generic terms
6. Plain-language explanation of non-coverage (not just SFIP quotes)
7. SFIP citations + web link
8. Policyholder Rights document (Appendix F) attached with every denial
9. Continue acknowledging coverage restrictions

### Appeals Process
- Policyholder may appeal to FEMA within **60 days** of denial.
- May file lawsuit against insurer within **1 year** of first written denial.
- Subsequent denial letters do NOT restart the 1-year statute of limitations.
- Lawsuit must be in US District Court for district where property is located.
- Neither FEMA nor WYO can extend the 1-year period.

### Overpayment Recovery
- WYO must reimburse FEMA for overpayments whether or not recovered from policyholder.
- WYO must respond within **30 days** of written notification.
- May appeal overpayment finding within **30 days**; max **60 days** from notification for appeal.

---

## 15. Salvage Rules

- Insurer always has right to seek salvage or take possession of damaged property.
- Adjuster must address salvage potential on **every** claim.
- Items wholly/substantially destroyed beyond repair = no salvage value (document in narrative).
- Items retained by policyholder: agree on "buy-back" value, credit claim. Retained items not insurable/payable unless restored to pre-loss condition.
- Salvor may not take possession before loss is settled and agreed by policyholder and insurer.
- Salvor entitled to inspection fee if salvage offer turned down.
- Adjuster's fee based on gross loss (before depreciation, deductibles, salvage).
- Adjuster/adjusting firm may NOT act as salvor.

---

## 16. Remote Claim Adjusting

- Dwelling Form ONLY (not GP or RCBAP).
- Requires: adequate technology (smartphone/tablet + internet/cellular), property access, no substantial damage, timely loss reporting, clear flood cause, no third-party involvement (no public adjuster/attorney).
- Both policyholder and insurer must agree.
- Claims over **$25,000**: adjuster must inspect in person before final payment.
- Only adjusters with active registered FCN may remote adjust (no trainees).

---

## 17. GFIP (Group Flood Insurance Policy)

- Covers IHP award recipients for flood damage from presidential disaster declaration.
- Only NFIP Direct services GFIPs.
- Uses Dwelling Form with modifications.
- **$200 deductible** (separate for building and contents).
- Single coverage limit for building + contents combined (up to IHP max grant amount).
- Certificate holder chooses allocation between building and contents.
- Term: **36 months**, beginning 60 days after disaster declaration.
- Cannot be canceled by certificate holder; cannot be renewed.
- No ICC coverage under GFIP.
- No underwriting review performed.

## 18. Professional Services

### When to Engage Engineer
**Exterior**: foundation cracking/movement, piers/pilings out of plumb, scour/erosion, displaced veneer/siding, displaced exterior wall.

**Interior**: cracked/separated/uneven floors, bowed/bulged walls, significant cracks above doors/windows, moisture/hydrostatic pressure, uncertain floor elevation, flood-damaged tile on concrete substrate.

**General**: causation disputes, repair methodology questions, large commercial losses.

### Requirements
- Only NFIP insurer or vendor can hire professional services (not adjusters/firms).
- Must be licensed in state of property.
- Pre-inspection cost estimate required and approved before authorizing.
- Engineer of Record cannot be Engineer Intern (EI) or Engineer-in-Training (EIT).
- Report must NOT interpret SFIP terms, discuss coverage, or value building cost to repair.
- Insurer may NOT request changes to signed/sealed reports — must request separate addendum.
- Full-color copy of report and any addendum must be provided to policyholder.
- No lump sums in billing. Multiple engineers need pre-approval.
- Outside professional must be unbiased and independent.

## 19. Code of Conduct — Consequences

- Violation confirmed → removal/suspension from NFIP work for minimum **1 year**.
- If violation not amenable to remedy → **indefinite or permanent** FCN revocation.
- 10 business days to respond to allegation.
- 20 calendar days to appeal final determination to Assistant Administrator.
- No response within 10 days → FEMA may close investigation without appeal opportunity.
- Conflict of interest: cannot work as/for public adjuster while registered with NFIP.
- Cannot adjust claims for property owned by adjuster or immediate family.
- Cannot accept money from third parties to steer business.
- Cannot accept monetary/non-monetary incentives from policyholders.

## 20. Claims Oversight — Common Findings

### Estimate Errors
- Not line-by-line, room-by-room with unit costs
- Depreciation not applied line-by-line
- Rooms not described/identified or don't match building diagram
- Building items on contents estimate (or vice versa)
- RCV loss settlement qualifications not documented

### Documentation Errors
- Missing/improper denial letters
- Mortgagee not on payment
- No POL waiver for late filing
- No signed POL or POL values don't match settlement
- Insufficient photos

### Payment Errors
- Misapplication of building/contents coverage
- Deductible improperly applied or not applied
- Duplicate payments
- Items of property for post-FIRM elevated building not allowed
- ACV items paid at RC, or vice versa

### Time Standard Violations
- Payment delay exceeds 7 business days from examiner review
- Assignment not made within 1 business day
- Closing report not submitted within 45 calendar days of DOL
- Policyholder not contacted within 2 business days
- Preliminary report not received within 15 calendar days
