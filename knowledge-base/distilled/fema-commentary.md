# FEMA Commentary — Distilled

Interpretive rulings from the SFIP Commentary (October 2021 Dwelling Form). References are to the October 2022 Flood Insurance Manual and June 2023 Claims Manual.

---

## Section I: Agreement

### One Building Per Policy (I.E-F)
- The SFIP is not a blanket policy; it covers only the single building specified in the application.
- Multiple policies with building coverage cannot insure a single building, even through different NFIP insurers.

### Dwelling + RCBAP Combined Limit (I.G)
- A Dwelling Form policy may be issued to a unit owner in a condo building also insured under an RCBAP.
- **Combined benefit cap: $250,000** for a single unit across both policies.
- No duplication of benefits — items paid under RCBAP cannot also be claimed under Dwelling Form.

### Accuracy of Information (I.C.3)
- The NFIP can correct errors even after a loss.
- If information is **intentionally misleading**, the policy may be voided.

## Section II: Definitions

### Basement (II.C.5)
- **Sole criterion**: whether the floor is below ground level on **all sides**.
- How the space is used, designed, or listed by a real estate agent is irrelevant.
- Sump wells and elevator pits are **not** basements because they are not a floor level.

### Building (II.C.6)
- Requires two or more outside rigid walls and a fully secured roof, affixed to a permanent site.
- Excludes gas/liquid storage tanks, shipping containers, and recreational vehicles (except qualifying travel trailers).

### Direct Physical Loss By or From Flood (II.C.14)
- **No assumption of damage.** There must be evidence of direct, physical damage to covered property.

### Collapse or Subsidence (II.B.2)
- Must occur during a flood from overflow of inland or tidal waters.
- Land is excluded unless it directly supports the building's foundation.
- Does not cover any other earth movement or gradual erosion.

### Principal Residence (II.C.25)
- Policyholder or spouse must have lived at the dwelling for **at least 80%** of the 365 days before loss (or period of ownership if less than 365 days).
- Distinct from "primary residence" used for surcharge purposes under the Homeowner Flood Insurance Affordability Act of 2014.

### Valued Policy (II.C.30)
- The SFIP is **not** a valued policy. It is a direct physical loss policy — pays for actual damage, not agreed-upon total value.

## Section III: Property Insured

### Coverage A — Building Property

#### Additions and Extensions (III.A.2)
- Connected by rigid exterior wall, load-bearing interior wall, stairway, elevated walkway, or roof: may be separately insured if addition meets the building definition (II.C.6).
- Connected by a common interior wall that is **not** load-bearing: always part of the dwelling, cannot be separately insured.

#### Detached Garage (III.A.3)
- Coverage limited to **no more than 10%** of the building limit of liability.
- Only one detached garage covered when not separately insured.
- Must meet the building definition (II.C.6) and be designed for automobile storage (large enough opening, able to support weight).
- **Not covered** if used for residential, business, or farming purposes.

#### Materials and Supplies (III.A.4)
- Covers materials/supplies for construction, alteration, or repair while stored in a fully enclosed building at the described location or adjacent property.
- **Does not include tools** for construction. Tools may be covered under Coverage B if owned by the policyholder and located in a building at the described location.

#### Building Under Construction (III.A.5)
- Must have at least two rigid exterior walls and a fully-secured roof at time of loss for coverage to apply.
- If construction stops for **more than 90 days**, or lowest floor is below BFE: **deductible doubles** (2x the normal deductible).

#### Coverage A Only Items (III.A.7)
- Carpet: Coverage A only when permanently installed over **unfinished** flooring. Coverage B in all other cases.
- Refrigerator: an appliance that both refrigerates and freezes is a refrigerator (Coverage A). **Food freezers** (freeze-only) are Coverage B.
- Walk-in freezers: must be permanently installed or built-in to qualify as "walk-in."
- Water heaters: must be affixed to or inside the insured building (including attached utility closet), inside a detached garage, or inside any building that services the insured building.
- Plumbing fixtures: includes a faucet affixed directly to the plumbing line. A faucet mounted onto a sink (connected to plumbing but not directly affixed) is a finished fixture.

#### Basement and Below-BFE Coverage Limits (III.A.8)
- In post-FIRM elevated buildings below the lowest elevated floor, or in any basement regardless of zone, coverage is limited to a specific list of items installed in functioning locations.
- **Concrete slabs** in post-FIRM elevated buildings: not considered structural and not covered unless **6 inches thick with rebar driven into the foundation**.
- **Drywall in basements**: covered only as unfinished, unfloated, and not taped, nailed to framing.
- **Elevators/dumbwaiters/chairlifts** outside the building: must be attached directly to the building or to the **16 sq ft landing area** immediately in front of a building door. Stairlifts must be attached to an exterior staircase used for egress.
- **Elevator-related equipment** installed below BFE **after September 30, 1987**: not covered.

#### Clean-up (III.A.8.b)
- Only applies to property that is itself insured under III.A.8.
- Does not cover clean-up of excluded items, even if done to facilitate repair.
- Clean-up is **not** the same as debris removal (III.C.1).

### Coverage B — Personal Property

#### Inside a Building
- Personal property may be inside **any building** (meeting II.C.6 definition) at the described location.

#### Personal Property Must Be Secured (III.B.3)
- In buildings not fully enclosed, personal property must be secured to prevent flotation. If it floats out, it is **conclusively presumed** not reasonably secured — no coverage.

#### Tenant Coverage (III.B.6)
- Tenant with Coverage B: stove/range and refrigerator are covered. **Proof of ownership required.**
- The 10% limit of liability for tenant improvements does **not** apply to these appliances.
- Tenant improvements: covered up to **10% of the personal property limit**.
- Insurer must obtain the **lease agreement** to verify insurable interest before payment.
- No duplication of benefits with a building owner's policy.

#### Condo Unit Owner (III.B.7)
- Interior walls, floor, and ceiling not insured under the association's policy: covered up to **10% of the personal property limit**.

#### Special Limits (III.B.8)
- **$2,500 aggregate** per loss for: artwork, photographs, collectibles, rare books, autographed items, jewelry, watches, precious stones, gold/silver/platinum articles, furs, and business personal property.
- Excess loss on special-limit items **cannot** be used to reduce the personal property deductible.

#### Antiques (III.B.9)
- Paid at **functional value** only — not rarity-based value. Depreciation is not based solely on age or physical condition.

### Coverage C — Other Coverages

#### Debris Removal (III.C.1)
- Covers removal of non-owned debris on/in insured property AND debris of insured property anywhere.
- Self-performed work valued at **federal minimum wage**.
- **Not covered**: removal of non-insured property owned by the policyholder (e.g., yard debris, driveway debris); removal of non-insured items even to facilitate repair (e.g., carpet in a basement, plants/shrubs along perimeter for foundation access).
- Does not increase Coverage A or B limits.

#### Loss Avoidance — Sandbags, Supplies, Labor (III.C.2.a)
- Up to **$1,000** for costs to protect the building from flood or imminent flood danger.
- Covers only specifically listed items: sandbags/sand, fill for temporary levees, pumps, plastic sheeting, lumber.
- Self-performed work at **federal minimum wage**.
- Requires either: (1) general flooding condition in the area (flood need not reach the building), or (2) legally authorized evacuation/civil order.

#### Property Removed to Safety (III.C.2.b)
- Up to **$1,000** for reasonable expenses to move insured property away from flood danger.
- Coverage at the new location for **45 consecutive days** from the date of move.
- Property must be placed in a fully enclosed building or reasonably protected from elements.
- Must be placed **above ground level** or **outside the SFHA**.
- **No deductible** applied to costs of moving property to safety.
- Policyholder must itemize expenses with valid proof of payment.
- When threat of flood ends, coverage ceases.

#### Condominium Loss Assessments (III.C.3)
- Covers policyholder's share of assessments for direct physical flood damage to the unit or common elements.
- **Not covered**: assessments for non-covered property (cleanup, debris, sand, landscaping, decks, sidewalks, pools); assessments charged by governmental bodies; assessments resulting from the association's deductible; assessments for personal property/contents losses.
- If policyholder has no Coverage A, **no coverage** for building damage or common area assessments.
- Total combined payments from all NFIP policies for a single unit: **max $250,000**.
- Does not increase Coverage A limits.

### Coverage D — Increased Cost of Compliance (ICC)

- Up to **$30,000** toward bringing a flood-damaged building into compliance with state/local floodplain management laws.
- **Combined Coverage A + Coverage D cannot exceed $250,000** (Dwelling Form maximum).
- No separate deductible for ICC.
- **Not eligible**: condominium unit owner policies (but RCBAP covering the building is eligible), GFIP-insured buildings, emergency program communities, garages/carports.
- Eligible activities: elevation, floodproofing (non-residential only, or residential basements meeting 44 CFR 60.6(b)/(c)), relocation, demolition, or combination.
- Demolition payment includes clearing the site and discontinuing utility service.
- **Repetitive loss building** trigger: two flood losses in 10 years, each averaging repair cost >= 25% of market value, previous claim paid by NFIP.
- **Substantial damage** trigger: repair cost >= 50% of market value.
- Must complete mitigation **within 2 years** of the loss.
- **Not covered**: code upgrades not related to floodplain management (e.g., plumbing, electrical); compliance for additions/improvements made after the loss; pre-existing compliance obligations; rebuilding below BFE with a variance.
- ICC is **excluded** from the 80% insurance-to-value calculation for replacement cost eligibility.

## Section IV: Property Not Insured

### Buildings Over Water (IV.2)
- Building must be **entirely** (not mostly) in, on, or over water or seaward of mean high tide. Constructed or substantially improved after September 30, 1982.

### Open Structures / Boathouses (IV.3)
- If a boathouse is used as a dwelling, the **dwelling portion** is covered even if foundation/building elements are shared with the boathouse — provided it is not entirely in/on/over water.

### Recreational Vehicles (IV.4)
- Defined as: built on single chassis, 400 sq ft or less, designed to be self-propelled or permanently towable by light-duty truck, designed primarily as temporary living quarters.

### Self-Propelled Vehicles (IV.5)
- Generally excluded. Exception: vehicles not licensed for public roads that service the described location, or handicapped-assist vehicles while inside a building at the described location.

### Plants and Living Things (IV.6)
- Live plants: **not covered**, even when inside a building.
- Artificial plants used as interior decor: **covered** under Coverage B.

### Underground Structures (IV.8)
- Wells, septic tanks, and septic systems: **not covered**.

### Exterior Surfaces (IV.9)
- Walks, walkways, decks, driveways, patios: **not covered** whether roofed or not, if located outside the building's perimeter exterior walls.

### Fences and Retaining Walls (IV.12)
- Generally not covered. **Exception**: coverage may exist if the item directly supports and is integral to the building's foundation (even if it has a secondary purpose as a retaining wall). Engineer inspection should be requested.

### Aircraft and Watercraft (IV.13)
- **Exception**: remote-controlled boats, aircraft, and recreational drones/UAVs (not carrying people/cargo, not commercial) **are covered**.

### Hot Tubs, Spas, Pools (IV.14)
- Hot tubs/spas that are **not bathroom fixtures**: excluded, along with swimming pools and all their equipment, wherever located.

### Coastal Barrier Resources (IV.15)
- No coverage for buildings constructed or substantially improved after designation within CBRS boundaries or Otherwise Protected Areas (OPAs).

## Section V: Exclusions

### Additional Living Expenses (V.A.5)
- **Excluded entirely.** No temporary housing, mileage, meals, or increased utility expenses.

### Ordinance or Law Compliance (V.A.6)
- Cost of complying with any ordinance/law for construction, demolition, remodeling, renovation, or repair is excluded, **except** Coverage D (ICC).
- Non-flood-damaged property required to comply with codes is **not covered**. Example: replacing an undamaged interior HVAC unit to match a replaced exterior unit due to SEER-rating, refrigerant, or size changes — not covered even if required by code.

### Flood in Progress (V.B)
- No payment for a loss caused by a flood that was a continuation of a flood existing before coverage began (application date or loan closing date).

### Earth Movement (V.C)
- Excluded even if caused by flood.
- Includes earthquake, landslide, land subsidence, sinkholes, destabilization from subsurface water accumulation, gradual erosion.
- **Covered exceptions**: mudflow (II.B.1.c) and land subsidence from erosion along a shore (II.B.2).
- Soils saturated by flooding causing the building to sink or differentially move: **excluded** (intervening cause).
- Reasonable cost to **replenish soils** supporting the foundation, eroded suddenly by flood: **covered**.

### Water, Moisture, Mildew, Mold (V.D.4)
- Excluded when substantially confined to the dwelling or within the policyholder's control (design defects, plumbing failures, failure to maintain after flood recedes).
- **Exception**: covered when the policyholder is reasonably prevented access to promptly remove flood-damaged items and the delay causes additional damage beyond their control. Must include proper documentation.
- Pre-existing damage to structural building components: **not covered**.

### Water Backup, Sump Overflow, Seepage (V.D.5)
- Excluded **unless** there is a flood in the area and the flood is the **proximate cause**.

### Pressure or Weight of Water (V.D.6)
- Excluded unless there is a flood in the area and the flood is the proximate cause.

### Power, Heating, Cooling Failure (V.D.7)
- Excluded unless the failure results from direct physical flood loss to equipment **on the described location**.

### Pollutants (V.F)
- Dwelling Form and RCBAP: testing/monitoring of pollutants covered only **when required by law or ordinance**, without dollar limit.
- General Property Form: up to **$10,000** for pollution damage caused by or resulting from flood.

## Section VI: Deductibles

- **Separate deductibles** apply to building and personal property for each loss.
- Building under construction without two rigid exterior walls and a fully secured roof: **deductible is doubled**.
- **No deductible** for: loss avoidance measures (III.C.2), condominium loss assessments (III.C.3), or ICC (III.D).
- Excess loss on special-limit items (III.B.8) **cannot** reduce the personal property deductible.

## Section VII: General Conditions

### Pair and Set (VII.A)
- Applies when a damaged item is ruined and cannot be replaced individually, rendering the other item in the pair/set unusable or worthless.
- Insurer's option: pay replacement cost minus depreciation of the damaged item, or pay the fair proportional value of the damaged item relative to the set.

### Other Insurance (VII.B)
- If loss is also covered by non-NFIP flood insurance: NFIP pays proportionally.
- If other policy states it is excess: NFIP policy is **primary**.
- Condo unit with both Dwelling Form and RCBAP: Dwelling Form is **excess** over RCBAP.
- **Combined NFIP payment cap: $250,000** per single condo unit.

### Requirements in Case of Loss (VII.G)

#### Prompt Notice
- Policyholder must report loss to insurer **immediately**.
- Failure to provide timely notice can be a **basis for claim denial**.

#### Separate Damaged from Undamaged Property
- Policyholder's duty to separate and organize for adjuster examination.

#### Inventory of Damaged Property (VII.G.3)
- Must show quantity, description, actual cash value, and amount of loss.
- If removing flood-damaged property before adjuster inspection: **photograph items in original location** before moving.
- Do **not** dispose of non-perishable or non-hazardous property before adjuster inspection.
- Retain samples/swatches of carpeting, wallpaper, upholstery, window treatments, and high-value items.

#### Proof of Loss (VII.G.4)
- Must be submitted **within 60 days** of the loss (or any FEMA extension).
- Must be signed and sworn to by the policyholder (or Executor if deceased).
- Electronic signatures accepted.
- **Multiple proofs of loss allowed** — initial plus additional payment requests, each within the 60-day deadline or extension.
- The proof of loss is **not** the claim — one claim per flood event regardless of how many proofs of loss are submitted.

#### Estimate Requirements (VII.G.4.f, VII.H.2.d)
- Must include **specifications of damaged buildings and detailed repair estimates**.
- NFIP will **not accept** non-itemized, lump sum, or single-line estimates or invoices.
- "Costs" for personal property means: replacement with like kind and quality at current pricing, including sales tax, shipping, and product assembly.

#### Prior Flood Damage Repairs (VII.H.2.e)
- Policyholder must provide evidence that **all previous flood damage was repaired** — even damage unrepaired by a previous owner.
- Acceptable proof: receipts, canceled checks, pre-flood photographs (including social media), credit card/bank statements showing contractor payments, itemized contractor invoices.

### Adjuster Role (VII.G.8)
- Only the **NFIP insurer** has authority to approve or deny a claim.
- Adjusters are **not authorized** to approve, disapprove, or communicate claim decisions.

### Salvage (VII.L)
- Policyholder may keep damaged property; loss payment is reduced by salvage value.
- Buy-back must be fully disclosed as a credit in the content loss under the applicable line item.

### Appraisal (VII.M)
- Used **only** to resolve disputes about the **amount** of payment.
- **Cannot** be used to determine scope, coverage, or causation of damage.

### Suit Against Us (VII.O)
- Statute of limitations: **one year** from the insurer's **first** written denial. Subsequent denials do not restart it.
- Must be filed in U.S. District Court where the loss occurred.
- Neither FEMA nor the insurer may extend the one-year deadline.

### Loss Settlement Methods (VII.R)

#### Replacement Cost (VII.R.2)
- Applies to **single-family dwellings** that are the policyholder's principal residence **and** insured at 80% or more of full replacement cost (or NFIP maximum).
- Pays to repair/replace without depreciation — least of: building limit, replacement cost with like kind/quality, or amount actually spent.
- If rebuilt at new location: cost limited to what would have been incurred at the former location.
- When repair/replacement cost exceeds **$1,000 or 5%** of the building insurance amount: not payable until actual repair/replacement is completed.
- Policyholder may initially claim on ACV basis, then claim additional replacement cost within **180 days** of loss.
- "Full replacement cost" **excludes** footings, foundations, piers, supports below lowest basement floor, below-ground supports, and underground flues/pipes/wiring/drains.

#### Special Loss Settlement — Manufactured Homes (VII.R.3)
- Applies to manufactured/mobile homes and travel trailers that are: at least **16 feet wide**, at least **600 sq ft**, and principal residence.
- If totally destroyed or not economically feasible to repair: pays the lesser of (replacement cost or **1.5x ACV**) or the building limit.
- **Economically feasible test**: if ACV x 1.5 < ACV of repairs, then not economically feasible to repair.
- If partially damaged and economically feasible: settled under standard replacement cost rules.

#### Actual Cash Value (VII.R.4)
- Applies when replacement cost conditions are not met, and to: multi-family dwellings, non-exclusive-dwelling-use units, detached garages, personal property, appliances, carpets/carpet pads, outdoor equipment, abandoned property, non-principal residences.
- **Proportional settlement**: when insured below 80% of replacement cost and below NFIP maximum — pays the greater of ACV or the proportional amount (insurance carried / insurance required x repair cost).

### Mortgage Clause (VII.N)
- All known mortgagees must be listed on each building claim payment.
- Mortgagee retains right to payment even during foreclosure.
- If policy is cancelled/non-renewed, coverage continues for mortgagee for **30 days** after notification.

## Section VIII: Nullification, Cancellation, Non-Renewal

### Fraud (VIII.A)
- Policy is **void from the date of the fraudulent act** — not just prospectively.
- Voided policies cannot be renewed or replaced by a new NFIP policy.

### Non-Fraud Nullification (VIII.B)
- Policy void from inception if: community was not participating in NFIP, property not eligible, no insurable interest, payment did not clear.
- Full refund of premium/fees/surcharges, but any claim payments must be returned first.

### Physical Alteration (VIII.D.4)
- Policy cancelled if building is altered to become ineligible. Examples: removing walls or roof, increasing below-ground value to >50%, relocating entirely over water.

### Insurable Interest (VIII.B.1.c / VIII.D.2)
- Requires the individual/entity to derive a benefit from the property's preservation and suffer a loss from its destruction.
- Property owner, tenant, or mortgagee may qualify.

---

## Section IX-X: Miscellaneous

### Liberalization Clause (IX)
- Coverage broadening changes apply automatically if implemented within 60 days before or during the policy term. Does **not** apply retroactively to prior terms.

### Governing Law (X)
- NFIP is governed exclusively by federal law. **State insurance laws do not apply.**
