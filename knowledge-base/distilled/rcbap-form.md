# NFIP Standard Flood Insurance Policy -- RCBAP Form

The Residential Condominium Building Association Policy (RCBAP) covers an entire residential condominium building under a single policy held by the condo association. At least 75% of the building's floor area must be residential. This is the key structural difference from the Dwelling Form (individual owner) and General Property Form (commercial).

---

## Coverage A -- Building Property

### What is Covered

- The entire residential condominium building, **including all units and improvements within units**
- Additions/extensions connected by rigid exterior wall, solid load-bearing interior wall, stairway, elevated walkway, or roof (may be insured separately at insured's option)
- Additions attached by a common interior wall that is NOT a solid load-bearing wall are always part of the building and CANNOT be separately insured
- Building under construction/alteration/repair at described location
- Materials and supplies for construction/alteration/repair stored in a fully enclosed building at described location or adjacent property
- Manufactured homes and travel trailers (if in SFHA, must be properly anchored)

### Covered Building Fixtures (Coverage A Only)

- Awnings, canopies, blinds
- Carpet permanently installed over **unfinished** flooring
- Central air conditioners
- Elevator equipment
- Fire extinguishing apparatus, fire sprinkler systems
- Walk-in freezers, furnaces
- Light fixtures
- Outdoor antennas/aerials fastened to building
- Permanently installed cupboards, bookcases, paneling, wallpaper
- Pumps and pump machinery
- Ventilating equipment
- Wall mirrors (permanently installed)

### Unit-Level Fixtures (Covered Under Coverage A)

Built-in dishwashers, built-in microwave ovens, garbage disposals, hot water heaters (including solar), kitchen cabinets, plumbing fixtures, radiators, ranges, refrigerators, stoves.

**Key RCBAP distinction**: These unit-level fixtures are covered under the association's building policy, not under individual unit owner policies.

### Below-Lowest-Elevated-Floor / Basement Limitations

In elevated post-FIRM buildings in Zones A1-A30, AE, AH, AR, AR/A, AR/AE, AR/AH, AR/A1-A30, V1-V30, VE, or in any basement regardless of zone, Coverage A is limited to:

- Central air conditioners
- Cisterns and water in them
- **Drywall**: walls/ceilings in basement only -- unfinished, unfloated, not taped, cost of labor to nail to framing only
- Electrical junction/circuit breaker boxes, outlets, switches
- Elevators/dumbwaiters (related equipment NOT covered if installed below BFE after 9/30/1987)
- Fuel tanks and fuel
- Furnaces and hot water heaters
- Heat pumps
- Nonflammable insulation (basement only)
- Pumps/tanks for solar energy systems
- Stairways/staircases attached to building (not separated by elevated walkways)
- Sump pumps
- Water softeners, chemicals, water filters, faucets (integral to plumbing)
- Well water tanks and pumps
- Required utility connections for listed items
- Footings, foundations, posts, pilings, piers, foundation walls, anchorage systems
- Clean-up costs

### Building Under Construction Rules

- If not yet walled/roofed: covered only while work is in progress, or up to 90 continuous days after work halts
- NO coverage until walled and roofed if lowest floor is below BFE in applicable zones
- Floor measurement: bottom of lowest horizontal structural member in V zones; top of floor in A zones

---

## Coverage B -- Personal Property

### What is Covered

Personal property must be inside the fully enclosed building AND either:
- Owned by unit owners **in common** (undivided ownership interest), OR
- Owned solely by the condo association and used exclusively for association business

**RCBAP distinction**: This does NOT cover individual unit owners' personal belongings. Those require a separate Dwelling Form policy (RCBAP only covers association-owned or commonly-owned personal property).

### Items Covered Under Coverage B Only

- Portable/window AC units
- Carpet not permanently installed over unfinished flooring
- Carpet over finished flooring
- Clothes washers and dryers
- Cook-out grills
- Food freezers (not walk-in) and food in any freezer
- Outdoor equipment/furniture stored inside
- Ovens
- Portable microwave ovens and portable dishwashers

### Below-Lowest-Elevated-Floor / Basement Personal Property

Limited to (must be installed in functioning location, connected to power):
- Portable/window AC units
- Clothes washers and dryers
- Food freezers (not walk-in) and food in any freezer

### Special Limits -- $2,500 per loss

- Artwork, photographs, collectibles, memorabilia (porcelain figures, sports cards, etc.)
- Rare books, autographed items
- Jewelry, watches, precious/semiprecious stones, gold/silver/platinum articles
- Furs or articles where fur is principal value

### Antiques

Only **functional value** is paid -- no collector/antique premium.

---

## Coverage C -- Other Coverages

### Debris Removal
- Non-owned debris on/in insured property and owned debris anywhere
- Self-performed work valued at federal minimum wage
- Does NOT increase Coverage A or B limits

### Loss Avoidance -- Sandbags, Supplies, Labor ($1,000 limit)
- Sandbags/sand, fill for temp levees, pumps, plastic sheeting, lumber
- Self-performed work at federal minimum wage
- Requires: flood damage imminent to a person of common prudence AND either general flooding in area or official evacuation/civil order issued
- Does NOT increase Coverage A or B limits

### Loss Avoidance -- Property Removed to Safety ($1,000 limit)
- Reasonable expenses to move insured property to protect from flood
- Property covered at temporary location for 45 consecutive days
- Must be placed in fully enclosed building or reasonably protected
- Moved property must be above ground level or outside SFHA
- Does NOT increase Coverage A or B limits

---

## Coverage D -- Increased Cost of Compliance (ICC)

- **Limit: $30,000** (in addition to Coverage A limit, but combined A + D cannot exceed NFIP maximum)
- No separate deductible
- Eligible activities: elevation, floodproofing, relocation, demolition
- Floodproofing limited to: nonresidential structures, or residential with basements meeting 44 CFR 60.6(b)/(c)

### Eligibility Triggers
1. **Repetitive loss**: 2 flood losses in 10 years, average repair cost >= 25% of market value each time, previous claim paid by NFIP, and state/community has repetitive loss provision; OR
2. **Substantial damage**: single loss repair cost >= 50% of market value, with state/community substantial damage provision being enforced

### ICC Exclusions (RCBAP-specific)
- Assessments by condo association on individual unit owners for compliance costs are NOT covered under ICC
- Garages and carports excluded
- Must complete work as soon as reasonably possible, max 2 years
- Code upgrades not related to floodplain management excluded
- No payment until work is actually completed

---

## Property NOT Covered

- Personal property outside the fully enclosed building
- Building/contents located entirely in/on/over water or seaward of mean high tide (if built/improved after 9/30/1982)
- Open structures, boathouses, structures into which boats float
- Recreational vehicles (except qualifying travel trailers)
- Self-propelled vehicles/machines (EXCEPTION: unlicensed vehicles used to service location or assist handicapped, while inside building)
- Land, lawns, trees, shrubs, plants, crops, animals
- Currency, coins, deeds, securities, bullion, manuscripts, stamps, stored value cards, scrip
- Underground structures (wells, septic tanks/systems)
- Exterior walks, walkways, decks, driveways, patios (inside or outside roof coverage)
- Gas/liquid containers and tanks
- Buildings with >49% ACV below ground (unless lowest level at/above BFE and below ground only for energy-efficient insulation)
- Fences, retaining walls, seawalls, bulkheads, wharves, piers, bridges, docks
- Aircraft, watercraft, and their furnishings/equipment
- Hot tubs/spas (not bathroom fixtures), swimming pools and all pool equipment
- Personal property used for incidental commercial occupancy
- Coastal Barrier Resources Act properties

---

## Key Exclusions

### Covered Only If Flood Is Proximate Cause
- Sewer/drain backup
- Sump pump discharge/overflow
- Seepage/leakage on or through property
- Pressure/weight of water against structure

### Always Excluded
- Loss in progress at policy inception or when coverage added
- Earth movement (earthquake, landslide, subsidence, sinkholes, gradual erosion) -- except mudflow and flood-caused shoreline subsidence
- Ice pressure/weight, freezing/thawing
- Rain, snow, sleet, hail, water spray (as standalone causes)
- Water/moisture/mold from conditions substantially confined to building or within insured's control (design defects, equipment failure, failure to maintain after flood recedes)
- Power/heating/cooling failure (unless flood directly damaged that equipment on-site)
- Theft, fire, explosion, wind, windstorm
- Deliberate acts by insured
- Alteration significantly increasing flood risk
- Loss of revenue, profits, use, access, business interruption, additional living expenses
- Pollutant testing/monitoring (unless required by law)

---

## Deductibles

- Separate deductibles apply to building and personal property per loss event
- Building under construction without walls/roof: deductible is **2x** the normal deductible
- No deductible on loss avoidance measures or ICC

---

## Coinsurance (Building Only)

### Requirement
Insurance carried must be at least the lesser of:
- 80% of building replacement cost, OR
- Maximum NFIP coverage available

### Penalty Formula
If underinsured:
1. (Insurance carried / Required insurance) = ratio
2. Loss amount x ratio = adjusted loss
3. Adjusted loss - deductible = payment

### Replacement Cost Calculation for Coinsurance
- Include: all covered building property replacement cost
- Exclude: building property not covered under policy
- Include ONLY improvements installed by the condo association (not individual unit owner improvements for this calculation)

---

## Loss Settlement

### Three Methods

**1. Replacement Cost** (standard buildings, not manufactured homes/travel trailers)
- Repair/replace without depreciation deduction
- Paid at the least of: policy limit, replacement cost with like kind/quality, or amount actually spent
- **Must actually complete repair/replacement before RCV is paid**
- If rebuilt at different location: payment capped at what it would cost at described location

**2. Special Loss Settlement** (manufactured homes/travel trailers that are >= 16 ft wide and >= 600 sq ft)
- Total loss or not economically feasible to repair: lesser of replacement cost or 1.5x ACV, capped at policy limit
- Partial damage (economically feasible to repair): replacement cost rules apply

**3. Actual Cash Value** (replacement cost minus depreciation)
Applies to:
- All personal property
- Abandoned insured property remaining as debris
- Outside antennas/aerials, awnings, outdoor equipment
- Carpeting and pads
- Appliances
- Manufactured homes/travel trailers not meeting Special Loss Settlement size requirements

### Pairs and Sets
Insurer's option: pay cost to replace the lost/damaged article (less depreciation), OR pay fair proportion of total pair/set value.

---

## Documentation Requirements

### Proof of Loss (60 days after loss)
Signed and sworn statement including:
- Date and time of loss
- Brief explanation of how loss happened
- Insured's interest and interests of others in damaged property
- Details of other insurance covering the loss
- Changes in title or occupancy during policy term
- **Specifications of damaged buildings and detailed repair estimates**
- Names of mortgagees/lienholders
- Occupancy details at time of loss
- Inventory of damaged personal property

### Personal Property Inventory
- Quantity, description, actual cash value, and amount of loss
- Attach all bills, receipts, related documents

### Insurer May Request
- Complete inventory with quantities, costs, ACV or replacement cost, loss amounts claimed
- Written plans and specifications for repair
- Evidence that prior flood damage has been repaired

### RCBAP-Specific Documents
Insurer may examine:
- Condominium Declarations
- Articles of Association or Incorporation
- Bylaws
- Rules and regulations
- All books of accounts, bills, invoices, vouchers

---

## Other Insurance Rules

- If other non-NFIP flood insurance covers same loss: NFIP pays proportional share based on coverage amounts
- If other policy declares itself excess: RCBAP is primary
- **If a unit owner has their own NFIP flood policy covering same loss: the RCBAP is primary**

This last point is critical for adjusters -- the association's RCBAP takes precedence over individual unit owner dwelling policies when both cover the same loss.

---

## Key RCBAP Differences from Dwelling and General Property Forms

1. **Single policy covers entire building** including all units and unit improvements -- not per-unit
2. **Coverage B is association/common property only** -- does not cover individual unit owners' personal belongings
3. **Coinsurance calculation** includes only association-installed improvements, not individual owner improvements
4. **ICC excludes assessments** levied on individual unit owners by the association
5. **Other insurance**: RCBAP is primary over individual unit owner NFIP policies
6. **75% residential threshold** -- building must be at least 75% residential floor area to qualify
7. **Unit-level fixtures** (appliances, cabinets, etc.) are covered under the association's building policy rather than individual dwelling policies
